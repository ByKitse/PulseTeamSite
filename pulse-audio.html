01 The 60-second read

Dipsea is the HBO of audio romance — closed-shop, pro-produced, prestige. Quinn is the TikTok of audio romance — celebrity-narrated thirst, second-person POV, creator-driven. Neither has video. Neither has Latina or Asian-language originals. Neither has interactive or co-listen. Pulse can own all three.
— The strategic frame

Both platforms exploded during 2020 lockdowns and have grown ~50× listening hours since 2021. The audio erotica category is now a defined niche inside the $56.6B digital adult content market, riding the BookTok / romantasy cultural engine. The interesting white space is everywhere they aren't.

The single most important data point

Ember & Ice (Quinn's MM fae princes Original narrated by Hudson Williams & Connor Storrie of Heated Rivalry) has hit 39 million minutes listened — Quinn's most-listened series ever. 03 Dipsea — Deep Dive

The premium incumbent. Now a RevenueCat "subscription lab."

Business

  • Founded: 2018, San Francisco · Gina Gutierrez (CEO), Faye Keegan (CTO)
  • Funding: ~$13.6–15.1M across three rounds. Last raise $8.2M (March 2021).
  • Acquisition: RevenueCat, Sept 26, 2024 — all-cash, undisclosed amount. Investors fully bought out.
  • ARR at acquisition: ~$6M / ~$505K monthly
  • Subs at acquisition: ~94K paying
  • Team: Was 29 mid-2024, now ~7 post-acquisition (subsidiary)
  • Pricing: $12.99/mo or $69.99/yr (~$5.83/mo). 7-day free trial. Frequent 50% promos.

Content model

  • 1,000+ titles — closed catalog, in-house writers + pro voice actors only
  • Series-based — many multi-chapter arcs (20+ chapters)
  • Categories (live on dipseastories.com/discover): Contemporary, Fantasy, Historical Romance, Audio Drama, Full Cast, Paranormal, Sapphic, Queer, M/M, M/F/M, Why Choose, Enemies to Lovers, Friends to Lovers, Second Chance, Slow Burn
  • Sub-tags: Scottish/Irish/British Accent, Western, Werewolves, Vampires, Fae, Greek Mythology, Regency, Extra Spicy, BDSM, Summer Heat, Love Triangle, Black Voices, Sports, Campus Romance, Boyfriend Roleplay
  • Featured collections: New Releases, Overheard Audios (no-dialogue sensual audio), "Top Characters + You" (interactive), Queer Romance, Extra Spicy & BDSM, Sleep Scenes & Stories, Wellness & Education
  • Production: full sound design, music, ambient — NOT celebrity-led
  • Cadence: weekly new chapter drops on flagship serialized series

04 Quinn — Deep Dive

The TikTok-native challenger. Celebrity-narrated, second-person, viral.

Business

  • Founded: 2019 (app launched 2021), LA · Caroline Spiegel (CEO, sister of Snap's Evan Spiegel)
  • Funding: ~$10M total. Investors: Day One Ventures, TQ Ventures (Scooter Braun), M13 founder Courtney Reum, Great Oaks, Global Asset Capital, Adrian Grenier
  • Subscribers: 300K+ (Fox Business) · 75%+ female
  • ARR: ~$12M+ (Variety/Marie Claire 2025–2026)
  • Listening hours: 3.2M minutes/year (2021) → 14M+ minutes/month (2023). ~50× growth.
  • Team: ~20 (doubled in past year)
  • Pricing: $7.99/mo or $59.99/yr. Price increases caused review backlash late 2025.

Content model

  • Hybrid: long-tail moderated UGC creators + premium "Quinn Originals"
  • Creator program: auditions, background checks, all submissions reviewed pre-publish
  • Creator monetization: subscription revenue share + "Roses" tipping (every listener gets 1 free Rose)
  • Categories (live on tryquinn.com): Boyfriend (#1 most popular), Friends to Lovers, Enemies to Lovers, Best Friend's Brother, Professor/Student, Roommates to Lovers, Strangers to Lovers, "Hold the Moan", MDom, FDom, MSub
  • Curated playlists: Best of All Time, Fan Favorites, Pride, Trending
  • Active creators: Naudio, Cavern Kingston, Noble, Atlas, AugustInTheWinter, LonelyFans
  • Format POV: second-person "to you" — no female voice, lets listener self-insert (mirrors BookTok first-person POV preference)
  • Cadence: dozens of new stories/week from creator network · Originals released as multi-episode drops

08 Five white-space lanes for Pulse

Where Pulse can win — specifically, things neither Dipsea nor Quinn has.

LANE 01 · NATIVE FORMAT EDGE

Watch + Listen — vertical microdrama with audio-only cut

Pulse already produces vertical-video spicy microdrama. Release every episode as both video and an audio-only cut. Now you have two distribution surfaces, two monetization moments, two organic discovery channels — for the same script.

Why it winsNeither Dipsea nor Quinn has video. ReelShort/DramaBox don't have audio. You sit between both.
Production costMarginal — voice tracks already exist on set
TikTok playVertical clips drive both directions: TikTok → app for video; TikTok → audio for listen-only
Subscription wedge"Listen offline" is a premium tier feature
LANE 02 · DEMOGRAPHIC WHITE SPACE

Spanish, Tagalog, Vietnamese, Mandarin originals

Both Dipsea and Quinn are nearly 100% English. ReelShort and DramaBox have proven Latina and Asian audiences are voracious romance consumers — the same audience Pulse is already targeting. Launch Pulse Picante (Spanish), Pulse Bagong (Tagalog), Pulse Đêm (Vietnamese), Pulse 心动 (Mandarin).

Maps to memoryMarie's existing Latino + Asian microdrama research
Cost structureNative-language voice talent in LA is abundant + reasonable
DistributionSpotify Spanish romance podcasts have huge audiences with no premium competitor
Sponsor appealMulticultural beauty/wellness brands desperately want this audience
LANE 03 · UNDERSERVED COMMUNITIES

Queer-male and 50+ originals

Ember & Ice's 39M minutes proved MM demand explodes when treated with prestige. Dipsea has limited M/M; Quinn has Ember & Ice plus a couple sapphic Originals but the broader queer-male catalog is thin. The 50+ romance audience driving "later-in-life rom-com" sales on Kindle is invisible on both platforms.

LANE 04 · FORMAT INNOVATION

Choose-your-own + co-listen + live

Three format gaps that neither platform has solved:

  • Choose-your-own ending in vertical video — microdrama format already supports cliffhangers; just add branch logic
  • Co-listen mode with synchronized playback + shared pause for partnered listening. Bloom does couple-focused content but it's passive.
  • Live drops with chat — Quinn does scheduled Original premieres but no live event format with the cast/narrator present

Any one of these is a sticky retention feature; all three together is a moat.

LANE 05 · CREATOR ECONOMY + IP STUDIO HYBRID

The graduated-creator pipeline Quinn hasn't built

Quinn is curated UGC + celebrity Originals — but there's nothing between them. Dipsea is closed-shop pro. Pulse can operate a third model: tiered creator program where top vertical-microdrama writers/actors graduate into multi-episode IP arcs that Pulse owns and licenses outward.

Tier 1Open creator submission (Quinn-style)
Tier 2Pulse "Verified" — revenue share + production support
Tier 3Pulse Originals — Pulse owns IP, licenses to ReelShort / Spotify / TikTok
Licensing upsideBookTok-to-screen pipeline is undeveloped — vertical integration possible
Bonus monetization layer

Marie owns Kitsé. A story-paired-to-product line — audio drama released alongside an intimate product, candle, lingerie, or food drop — is a layer neither platform can replicate without retail. "Yes, Chef" already proved restaurant power-dynamic stories perform; pair one with a Kitsé chef supper club for the obvious crossover.

09 Recommended playbook

What to launch first. What to test. What to skip.

Phase 1 — Launch (next 90 days)

  • Pick 3 vertical microdrama series Pulse already has scripts for. Re-record them as audio-only cuts at the same time as the video shoot — incremental cost ~$5–10K/episode for cleaner sound design.
  • Release simultaneously: vertical app + Spotify podcast feed (audio-only). Spotify has zero gatekeeping for audio erotica, unlike Apple Podcasts.
  • Pricing: $8.99/mo or $59.99/yr — undercut Dipsea, match Quinn.
  • Audience seed: launch with one Spanish-language Original (Pulse Picante #1) to plant the demographic flag immediately.

Phase 2 — Differentiate (Q4 2026)

  • Ship co-listen mode — easiest retention feature to differentiate on. Both Spotify and Apple Music have synchronized playback patents you can license/work around.
  • Launch Pulse Originals slate: 4–6 multi-episode arcs with named talent. Target one MM original early to ride the Ember & Ice wave.
  • Open creator submissions — start the long tail. Background checks, revenue share, "Roses"-style tipping equivalent.

Phase 3 — Compound (2027)

  • Interactive / choose-your-own for top Originals — pre-built script trees turn into 4–8 endings per arc
  • License outward — Spotify Originals deal, Audible exclusive window, ReelShort co-production
  • Kitsé crossover — at least one "Yes, Chef"-style food-and-romance drop with a real Kitsé chef and a paired supper club
What to skip

Don't fight Dipsea on prestige longform serialized romance head-on — the catalog gap is too big and 1,000+ stories is years of writing. Don't fight Quinn on TikTok-celebrity-narrator hype — the casting Rolodex is built. Win the lanes neither of them is running.
