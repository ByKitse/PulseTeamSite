My Billionaire Dad Is Also My Boss

Season 2: "Salinas & Daughter"  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Family Drama / Corporate Thriller  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: Family drama / Corporate thriller

Season Arc: Catalina and Rodrigo are co-existing but not yet reconciled. A hostile takeover threat — backed by Catalina's estranged maternal uncle — forces them to work together. This season is about Catalina choosing what to do with grief, and Rodrigo learning it's too late to be her father but not too late to be something.

Key Characters: CATALINA (late 20s, fierce, carrying something she doesn't have a name for), RODRIGO (50s, publicly humbled, privately rebuilding), JAMES (Rodrigo's longtime assistant — loyal, perceptive, knows everything), UNCLE RAFAEL (Catalina's maternal uncle, 60s, powerful, doing this for the wrong reasons), SOFÍA (Catalina's late mother — appears in memory/voiceover only), PATRICIA (board member ally, 50s, Rodrigo's only real friend on the board)

Memory Protocol: SOFÍA appears only in memory flashbacks and described recollections — never in real time. These moments are italicized and clearly framed as memory. Episode 10 is the paywall episode.

Episode 1
The Board Reacts
Runtime: ~75s

INT. SALINAS GROUP BOARDROOM — MORNING

Emergency session. Rodrigo sits at the head of the table — different now, the invulnerability of last season stripped away. Half the board — led by PATRICIA, 50s, steady and unsentimental — accepts the reality: Catalina is acknowledged, the lawsuit is settled, life continues. The other half, led by FUENTES, 60s, smooth-voiced and precise, sees one thing: instability.

CATALINA sits against the wall as an observer — not at the table, not yet. She watches every face with the focused patience of a woman taking an inventory she intends to use later.

Fuentes
(to the room, measured)
"A family secret, a protracted lawsuit, an heir no one has vetted. The institutional uncertainty alone is sufficient to trigger a governance review."
Patricia
"She's been working here for eight months. Her performance record speaks for itself."
Fuentes
"Performance and succession are different questions."

After the session, Fuentes finds Rodrigo in the corridor. His voice is calibrated — almost friendly.

Fuentes
"We'll need to revisit the succession structure."
Rodrigo
"There's no succession discussion scheduled."
Fuentes
"There will be." He walks away.
⚡ Cliffhanger

James appears at Rodrigo's shoulder. "He made three calls from the boardroom during the session." Rodrigo: "To who?" James: "I'm working on that." A few steps behind: Catalina, watching Fuentes's retreating back. She hasn't been listening to Rodrigo. She's already on her phone — she started working on it herself the moment the session ended.

Episode 2
The Threat
Runtime: ~80s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — JAMES'S OFFICE — DAY

James lays out what he's found. Fuentes's calls went to a representative for Meridian Capital — a private equity firm known specifically for hostile acquisition, the kind that dismantles and resells. Meridian has been buying Salinas Group stock through shell companies for four days — quietly, in increments designed to avoid disclosure thresholds.

James
"The buying started four days ago. Before the board session. Before Rodrigo made the announcement." A pause. "This wasn't a reaction to the news. It was waiting for a trigger event. Any destabilizing event would have done it."
Catalina
"They had the infrastructure in place. They just needed an excuse."

That evening, Catalina stays late pulling Meridian Capital's public filings. Three holding companies, then four. She traces the ownership layer by layer. She prints the fourth layer. Walks to Rodrigo's office and sets the page on his desk without a word.

Rodrigo
(reading, his face going gray)
"Where did you find this?"
Catalina
"Public filing. Third shell company. The fourth layer is the primary investor." She sits. "I know who he is."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo looks up from the page. His expression is something Catalina hasn't seen on his face before — not fear, not calculation. Something older than that. "Catalina. This fund. The name." He sets the page down. "That's your mother's brother." The office is very quiet. Catalina looks at the page. She already knew. She has known since the second holding company. She just needed him to say it out loud.

Episode 3
The Uncle
Runtime: ~80s

INT. CATALINA'S APARTMENT — PHONE CALL — EVENING

Rafael Montoya calls Catalina directly. Not Rodrigo — Catalina. He has a warm voice, the kind that sounds like family even when it isn't acting like it.

Rafael
"Your mother spent thirty years in that man's shadow. You don't owe him anything."
Catalina
"I know that."
Rafael
"Help me take the company. You keep half. Walk away from him clean. This is what your mother would have wanted."
Catalina
(a pause — precise)
"My mother has been gone for four months. You didn't do this for her. You started building this position eight months ago — I checked the filing dates. She was still alive." A beat. "You did this for you."
Rafael
(quietly)
"Your mother asked me to protect you. That's what I'm doing."
Catalina
"By tearing down the only institution where I can find out what really happened to her career? By making sure I inherit nothing but a burn site?"
⚡ Cliffhanger

A silence on the line. Then Rafael says the one thing she wasn't braced for. "Your mother wrote you a second letter, Catalina. I have it. I've had it for four months." A breath. "Come see me — in person — or you'll never know what's in it." He hangs up. Catalina sits with the dead phone against her ear for a long moment. Then she sets it down. Her expression gives nothing away. But her hand is not entirely steady.

Episode 4
The Document
Runtime: ~85s

INT. RAFAEL'S OFFICE — DAY

Rafael's office is expensive and uncomfortable in the way that powerful men make rooms uncomfortable — everything in its place, nothing warm. He makes Catalina wait. She sits. She doesn't check her phone. She reads the room instead — the filing system, the desk, the case stacked beside the coffee table. An assistant delivers water and leaves a folder on the table by accident. Or not by accident. The assistant doesn't come back for it.

Catalina reads it. Inside: a memo on Rodrigo's personal attorney's letterhead. Dated twenty years ago. Subject: Catalina Montoya, age eight. Confirmed location. Confirmed identity. No action recommended. Rodrigo knew. Not for eight years — for twenty.

Catalina
(to herself, barely)
"Twenty years."

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — AFTERNOON

Catalina enters without knocking. Sets the memo on his desk. Rodrigo reads it. He doesn't deny it. He doesn't reach for an explanation. He looks at her — just looks at her, directly, with nothing shielding his face.

Catalina
(quietly, very controlled)
"Twenty years."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo says nothing. He opens his mouth once and closes it. There is nothing to say — not defensible, not explainable, not reducible to anything that could help him right now. Catalina turns and walks out. He doesn't call after her. For the first time in his professional life, Rodrigo Salinas does not reach for the next move. He just sits in the silence she's left behind.

Episode 5
The Rupture
Runtime: ~80s

INT. CATALINA'S APARTMENT — DAYS 1 THROUGH 5

Five days. Catalina doesn't come to the office. She doesn't call. She goes through her mother's things — a project she has been avoiding for four months. She finds notebooks, annotated articles, research that was never credited. Her mother's handwriting in the margins of Salinas Group annual reports from 2003, 2004, 2005. Watching. Tracking. Never named.

Day four: Rodrigo sends one text. "I understand if you don't come back. But the company needs you this week. Not me — the company." She reads it. Does not respond. Day five: PATRICIA calls.

Patricia
"I've known Rodrigo for twenty-two years. He has done an extraordinary amount of damage in that time. He also built something real. I'm not asking you to forgive him. I'm asking you to decide whether what he built is worth saving — because Fuentes and your uncle are going to dismantle it in pieces and four thousand people will lose their jobs and their healthcare."
Catalina
(a pause)
"I'm aware of the headcount, Patricia."
Patricia
"I know you are. That's why I called you."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina hangs up. Sits still for a long moment. Opens her laptop. James's video call icon appears — he's been online, waiting, for two hours. "Tell me what I need to know about the Meridian position." James exhales audibly: "Thank god." He starts pulling documents. In the background, visible over his shoulder: Rodrigo passes the open door of James's office. He sees the screen. He sees Catalina. He keeps walking. He does not stop. But he slows — just for one step.

Episode 6
Rodrigo Acts
Runtime: ~85s

INT. SALINAS GROUP BOARDROOM — SECOND EMERGENCY SESSION

Rodrigo walks in with his personal attorney and a box of documents. No prepared remarks. He opens the box and lays files on the table without preamble.

Rodrigo
"I did not do the right thing. I did the easiest thing — the thing that cost me least and cost Sofía Montoya most. What is in these files is what I did in the dark when I could not do the right thing in the light." He doesn't look away from the table. "Anonymous trust payments to Sofía's estate. A college fund opened when Catalina was nine. Ten years of consulting fees paid under a pseudonym because I couldn't formally credit the work I'd taken from her mother — but I couldn't stop paying it back either. These are not a defense. They are a record. My daughter deserves to know the full truth."

Silence. Patricia speaks first.

Patricia
"Fuentes. Do you still intend to pursue the succession review?"

Fuentes looks at the documents. Looks at Rodrigo. Something in his calculation shifts.

Fuentes
"I'll need to consult with Meridian Capital."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Patricia opens her own folder. "I think you'll find they're no longer interested in the position." Every head turns to her. "I bought their stake this morning. Through my private office." She closes the folder. "The board has a new majority shareholder." She looks across the table at Catalina — who came back this morning and is sitting at the table, not against the wall. "The floor is yours, if you want it."

Episode 7
What He Did
Runtime: ~80s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — EVENING

The Meridian threat is dead. Fuentes resigns the next morning — a brief statement, no explanation. The building is quiet at 7 PM. Catalina comes in without knocking. She has the full payment records from the attorney's files — thirty years of anonymous compensation printed out and organized. She's done the math. She sets the calculation on his desk.

Catalina
"It's more than the lawsuit was worth. Combined."
Rodrigo
"I know."
Catalina
"I'm not here to relitigate it. I've done that internally for four months." A beat. "What did my mother's letter say? The second one. Rafael has it. He told me she wrote one."

Rodrigo goes very still. His expression does something complicated.

Rodrigo
"What letter?" A pause — and then something on his face changes. "Your mother and I spoke. Once more. Four months before she died. She called me. We talked for almost two hours." He stands up. "She didn't tell you." It's not a question. "What did Rafael tell you she wrote?"
⚡ Cliffhanger

"That she wrote me a letter and gave it to him to deliver in person." Rodrigo moves to the window. His back is to her. "If Sofía wrote you something — if she gave it to Rafael — it wasn't instructions to accept my repayments. She would never have done that." He turns. "She was protecting you from something. I don't know what. But a letter held for four months by a man who used it as leverage is not what your mother intended." He looks at Catalina. "Don't go back to him alone."

Episode 8
The Uncle's Gambit
Runtime: ~80s

INT. CONFERENCE HALL — BACKSTAGE — MORNING

Rafael's financial attack has failed. So he goes to the press. A long interview — specific, credible, 70% accurate and shaped to look like 100%. Rodrigo's crimes against Sofía. Catalina's vulnerability. The exploitation of a dying woman's trust. It runs the morning Catalina is scheduled to speak at a corporate governance conference. She gets the alert twenty minutes before she walks onstage.

Backstage. James beside her, article on his phone. She reads it. A PR handler materializes.

PR Handler
"We can pull you. Reschedule. Issue a statement first, control the frame—"
Catalina
"No."

She hands the phone back to James. Adjusts the collar of her jacket. Checks her reflection in the dark screen of a monitor. Her face is completely composed.

James
"What are you going to say?"
⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina takes a breath. "The truth." She walks toward the stage entrance. James watches her go — this woman who arrived eight months ago demanding her father see her, who has spent every day since being tested and not breaking and learning to use every room she walks into. The stage light hits her. She steps forward.

Episode 9
Her Statement
Runtime: ~85s

INT. CONFERENCE HALL — MAIN STAGE — CONTINUOUS

Catalina sets aside her prepared remarks. The room is 800 people. She speaks for eight minutes. No notes. She talks about her mother — what it cost a woman in 1995 to have her work stolen with no recourse, no visibility, no legal protection. What it cost a daughter to grow up without a father who chose not to show up. What she found when she started working for him — the payments, the silence, the incomplete repentance. What she is still deciding.

Catalina
"I haven't forgiven my father. I don't know yet if I will. That is my decision to make — not my uncle's, not the press's, not this room's. What I know is this: there are four thousand people whose healthcare and livelihoods depend on this company functioning. My mother worked every day of her life. I am my mother's daughter. So I'm going to work."

Eight hundred people. Silence first — then the kind of applause that comes from something being true.

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — SAME TIME

Rodrigo watches the conference stream on his laptop. When she finishes he sits back. James, in the doorway, says: "She's going to be extraordinary." Rodrigo watches his daughter at the podium — precise, fierce, made from everything he failed to be on time.

⚡ Cliffhanger

"She already is." He picks up his phone. His attorney is calling. "Rodrigo. The board has been in contact. Patricia wants to move forward with the co-CEO structure. They want a vote this week." He sets the phone down. Looks at the screen — Catalina, still at the podium, taking questions, answering each one with the directness he taught himself and she inherited without his help. "Schedule it," he says. "Whatever she decides."

Episode 10
The Vote 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

INT. SALINAS GROUP BOARDROOM — DAY

The vote is 8 to 1. Fuentes is gone. The lone no is a board member whose contract expires in six months. Patricia presents the co-CEO resolution — equal authority, two-year trial, shared signature rights. Catalina signs it at the head of the table, Rodrigo beside her. Neither speaks during the signing. The board files out.

They are alone in the boardroom. For the first time since this began — truly alone, no James at the door, no agenda item pending. Catalina looks at her signature on the document. Rodrigo looks at it too.

INT. SALINAS GROUP — LOBBY — MINUTES LATER

Rafael is in the lobby. He came in person, unannounced. He looks exhausted — not defeated, but emptied of something he had been carrying for a long time. He holds out an envelope. Cream paper. Her mother's handwriting on the front: CATALINA. Her full name, both names, the way Sofía always wrote it.

Rafael
"She made me promise to deliver it in person. She made me promise not to use it as leverage." He meets Catalina's eyes. "I broke that promise. I'm sorry."

Catalina looks at the envelope. At her name in her mother's handwriting. She reaches out and takes it.

Catalina
"Rafael." He waits. "Did she know? At the end. Did she know I'd be okay?"
⚡ Cliffhanger

Rafael's eyes fill. "She said — she said you were the one thing she never worried about. She said you always knew what to do." Catalina looks at the envelope. Her mother's handwriting. Her full name. She turns and walks to the window — the city below, the building she has half-inherited, the life she is still writing. She opens the envelope alone. We do not see what's inside. We see her face as she reads. Something in it — the thing she has been holding for nine months — finally moves.

Episode 11
Plan B
Runtime: ~85s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — 3 AM

Three weeks after the vote. Rafael, furious and humiliated, has leaked Salinas Group's proprietary procurement model to a competitor — corporate espionage, a federal matter. The damage is real and must be contained within 72 hours. Catalina and Rodrigo have been in the office since 9 PM. James brings coffee at midnight and leaves without being asked. At 3 AM the fix is in place.

Rodrigo
"You should sleep."
Catalina
"You should sleep."
Rodrigo
"I'm used to nights like this."
Catalina
(looking at the window)
"So am I. My mother worked nights. She always had music on." She pauses. "Something mid-tempo. Something from when she was young."

Rodrigo is quiet for a moment. Then he reaches across the desk to a small radio — old, analog, one good station. He turns it on. Something warm and mid-tempo fills the room. Catalina looks at it.

Catalina
"She had one just like that."
⚡ Cliffhanger

"I know," he says. Simply. "I gave it to her. 1991. Before everything went wrong." He looks at his hands. The radio plays. Catalina looks at it for a long time. Then at him. She doesn't say anything. Neither does he. The station plays on. The city is quiet outside. In the silence between them — not comfortable yet, not uncomfortable either — something new is being built, one hour at a time.

Episode 12
Salinas & Daughter
Runtime: ~90s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — A THURSDAY AFTERNOON

Rafael has been arrested. He pleaded to a lesser charge and cooperated fully — he has nothing left to protect. The company is stable. Patricia has quietly restructured the board so that no single hostile actor can replicate what Fuentes and Rafael attempted. The building is calm. On a Thursday afternoon — no occasion, no meeting — Rodrigo calls Catalina into his office. He slides a folder across the desk.

Catalina
"What is this?"
Rodrigo
"New letterhead. Effective next week."

She opens the folder. At the top of the page, in the clean serif font of the company's official identity: SALINAS & DAUGHTER. Her name on the left. His on the right.

Catalina
"You should have put my name first."
Rodrigo
(a beat — then quietly)
"It was going to be DAUGHTER & SALINAS. James talked me out of it."
James
(from the doorway, unapologetic)
"I stand by that decision."

Catalina looks at the letterhead. The room is quiet. Something in her face — the thing she has been carrying since she walked into this building, since her mother died, since she was eight years old and her father was thirty miles away — shifts. Not resolved. Not erased. But different. Lighter, in the specific way of weight that has been named.

⚡ Cliffhanger

She takes the letterhead. Pulls the chair across from his desk and sits — not as an employee, not as an adversary. As something else. Something she is still finding the name for. She picks up her pen. Signs her name at the bottom of the page — her full name, both halves, Catalina Montoya Salinas — and caps the pen. Slides it back. "Don't make me regret this." He looks at her signature. The full name. Both names. He says nothing. But for the first time since she walked into his boardroom nine months ago, he smiles. She looks at him. And she almost — almost — smiles back.
