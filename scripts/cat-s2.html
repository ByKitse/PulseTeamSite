The Cat Who Knew Too Much

Season 2: "The Copy Cat Killer"  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Animal Drama / Mystery-Thriller  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: AI-generated animal drama / Mystery-Thriller

Season Arc: Luna is now living with Detective Marcos. A second cat, Felix, enters the picture. The DA is corrupt. Marcos goes rogue. This season reveals the scheme was bigger than one murder — and Luna's role in the solution is even more pivotal.

Animal V.O. Protocol: LUNA (Siamese cat) has full internal V.O. narration — italicized, labeled LUNA (V.O.). Luna does NOT speak to humans. All communication is through body language. FELIX has no V.O. — he communicates only through deliberate action.

Key Characters: LUNA (Siamese cat, our V.O. hero), MARCOS (Detective, now suspended), FELIX (sleek black cat, belongs to no one), THE DA (District Attorney, corrupt), VERA (hidden witness, former law firm employee, 40s), DAVID (still in prison), SERENA (cooperating witness)

Escalation Note: Cliffhangers intensify with each episode. Episode 10 is the paywall episode — the season's most devastating turning point.

Episode 1
Not the First
Runtime: ~75s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — EVIDENCE BOARD — NIGHT

Marcos's apartment wall is covered in photographs, strings of red thread, cold case files spread across the kitchen table. He pulls a thick manila folder — stamped CLOSED — and opens it. Luna sits on the arm of the couch, watching his face.

Luna (V.O.)
"He hadn't slept. I could tell by the way he moved — deliberate, like a man walking through water. When Marcos gets very still, he's found something. He was very still."

Marcos lays three case files side by side. Three women. Three deaths — ruled accidental. Three large life insurance payouts to male beneficiaries. Two years apart each time.

Marcos
(to himself, barely audible)
"Same pattern. Same staging. Same insurer. He did this before Elena. Twice."
Luna (V.O.)
"Two women before Elena. Two lives erased. And no one connected them because the cats were gone — rehomed, surrendered, disappeared. All except one."

Marcos pulls a photo from the third file. A sleek black cat. Property photo from the crime scene of victim number two — the cat sitting in the window. Marcos reads the intake note clipped to it: "Cat adopted by DA Richard Harlan. Personal request."

Marcos
(quietly)
"The DA personally adopted the dead woman's cat."

Luna stands up on the couch arm. Marcos turns and looks at her — really looks — processing. He pins the photo of the black cat next to a photo of Luna on his board. He steps back. His face changes.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos looks between the two photographs — Luna's pale Siamese face, and the black cat's unreadable stare — and says quietly: "Two cats. Two cases. Same killer."

Episode 2
Felix
Runtime: ~75s

EXT. CITY STREETS — CRIME SCENE LOCATIONS — VARIOUS — DAY

Marcos photographs a black cat at the corner of Vela Street — the same block as victim number one's house. The cat sits perfectly still, watching him, then vanishes around the corner. Two days later: the same cat, outside victim number two's former apartment building. Marcos's photos line up. It's the same animal.

Luna (V.O.)
"He was mapping it. The way I had mapped the townhouse, the shelter, the streets between. He was doing what I do. And he was doing it on purpose."

Luna paces the apartment windowsill every evening at the same hour, staring down at the street. Marcos notices. He stops telling her to come down.

Marcos
(to Luna, studying her posture)
"You know him, don't you. You've seen him before."
Luna (V.O.)
"I didn't know him. But I recognized what he was. A witness. Like me."

Early morning. A sound outside the apartment door — soft, deliberate. Luna goes rigid. Marcos opens the door. The black cat — Felix — sits in the hallway, perfectly composed, staring directly at Luna through the open door. Neither cat moves. The city hums below.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Felix holds Luna's gaze for three full seconds — then turns and walks deliberately down the hall, reaches the stairwell door, and looks back once over his shoulder. Clearly expecting to be followed.

Episode 3
The DA's Secret
Runtime: ~80s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — LAPTOP — NIGHT

Marcos works through public financial filings — DA Harlan's office disclosures, property records, shell company registrations. Luna sits beside the laptop, watching the screen as if she can read it.

Luna (V.O.)
"He was following the money the way I follow scent — layer by layer, deeper, until something true surfaces."
Marcos
(under his breath)
"Harlan Development LLC... registered to a Thomas Harlan. That's his brother." He leans back. "Six wire transfers. Same dates as David's case milestones."

Marcos pushes back from the table. Gets up. Paces. Luna drops off the desk and moves to the radiator in the corner — an old cast-iron unit she ignores most evenings. Tonight she sniffs the gap behind it. Digs her paw in. Something slides out.

Marcos
(crossing the room)
"What did you find?"

A prepaid burner phone. Dusty. Previous tenant's, or deliberately hidden. Marcos powers it on. One contact saved in the address book — no name, just a number. He runs it. His face goes flat.

Marcos
(quietly)
"That's the DA's private cell. His direct line. Nobody has that number."

The burner phone sits on the kitchen table between them. Then it vibrates. The screen lights up: UNKNOWN. Marcos and Luna both go completely still, staring at it.

⚡ Cliffhanger

The phone buzzes a second time. Neither Marcos nor Luna moves. The screen glows UNKNOWN in the dark of the apartment — and outside the window, somewhere on the street below, a car idles without turning off.

Episode 4
Internal Affairs
Runtime: ~80s

INT. INTERNAL AFFAIRS OFFICE — DAY

A spare, fluorescent room. Marcos sits across from two IA investigators. He's laid out what he has — the financial connections, the cold case pattern, the burner phone. The investigators exchange a look he doesn't like.

IA Investigator
(measured)
"Detective Marcos. DA Harlan has served this city for sixteen years. What you're describing — shell companies, a borrowed phone — that's circumstantial at best."
Marcos
"It's a pattern. Three women. Three payouts. The same man got all three cases closed."
IA Investigator
"Stand down. Do not pursue this further. That is a direct instruction."

Marcos stands down — officially. He walks back to the precinct. His badge and gun are on his captain's desk when he arrives. A note: SUSPENDED PENDING REVIEW. He picks up the note. Sets it back down. Walks out.

Luna (V.O.)
"He came home at 3 PM on a Tuesday and sat in the dark for twenty minutes. I let him. Then I climbed onto his lap. There are things you cannot fix and things you can. I can do this."

Luna settles on Marcos's lap. He strokes her once. Then looks at her — really looks.

Marcos
(quietly)
"Well. I guess it's just us now."

Luna headbutts his chin with force. Then turns and looks pointedly at the door.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos follows her gaze to the door — and on the floor, barely visible under the gap: a folded piece of paper, just slid through. He unfolds it. One address. Tonight's date. A time: 9 PM. No signature.

Episode 5
Cats Don't Need Badges
Runtime: ~85s

EXT. DA HARLAN'S TOWNHOUSE — NIGHT

Marcos stands across the street. Through the lit windows of the DA's townhouse: movement, voices, a dinner party in full swing. He can't go in. No badge, no warrant, no legal standing. He looks down. Luna sits at his feet. Felix materializes from the shadows beside her.

Luna (V.O.)
"He had the address but not the access. We had the access. The ground-floor window — left an inch open for the party's heat. I had measured it from the street."
Marcos
(crouching, absurd, whispering)
"I can't tell you two to go in there. I'm not telling you that. I'm not — okay. I'm going to sit here and not look directly at whatever happens next."

Luna and Felix cross the street. Felix leads — he knows this house. Through the low window, into the ground floor. Felix moves with the certainty of an animal who has been inside before. Luna follows, reading the rooms — guests in the dining room, the DA's laugh booming from behind closed French doors.

Luna (V.O.)
"He was laughing. Loudly. Comfortably. He had no idea we were there. That was the advantage of being small and silent and beneath his notice."

Felix stops in the study at the far end of the hall. Sits. Faces the north wall. Doesn't move — the exact posture of an animal that has done this before. Luna moves alongside him. She presses her paw against the bottom corner of a large oil painting. The frame swings slightly outward — hinged.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Behind the painting: a wall safe, flush-mounted, brushed steel, combination lock. Felix sits in front of it without moving — and from his posture, from the absolute stillness of him, Luna understands: he has been waiting to show someone this for a very long time.

Episode 6
The Safe
Runtime: ~80s

INT. DA HARLAN'S TOWNHOUSE — STUDY — NIGHT

Marcos slips inside through the side door — a neighbor's spare key, borrowed on a story about a gas leak check. He moves through the house like smoke, past the laughter of the dining room, into the study. Luna and Felix are waiting in front of the painting.

Luna (V.O.)
"He saw it immediately. The safe. The hinge on the painting. He looked at Felix and then at me and he didn't say a word."

Marcos works the combination with practiced hands — three attempts, each slower and more precise. The mechanism clicks on the third. He opens the safe. Inside: a black hard drive and a legal envelope thick with documents. He photographs everything, pockets the drive.

Marcos
(barely breathing)
"Audio recordings. That's a recording device. He's been keeping insurance on everyone he's ever bribed. Six years of it."

A floorboard. Heavy steps. At the end of the hallway — the DA's bodyguard, a broad shape in a dark jacket, speaking into a radio.

Bodyguard
(into radio)
"We've got movement in the study. Someone's in the house."

Marcos runs. Luna bolts left. Felix bolts right — back into the interior of the house.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos clears the front door and hits the sidewalk at a run — the hard drive in his jacket — but when he looks back through the window, Felix is still inside, a small black shape frozen at the far end of the hallway, the bodyguard between him and every exit.

Episode 7
Viral
Runtime: ~80s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — MORNING

Felix appears at the apartment door at 7 AM — thin, composed, entirely uninjured. He squeezed out through the mail slot. Marcos exhales. Luna sniffs Felix once and looks away, satisfied.

Luna (V.O.)
"I knew he'd get out. He'd gotten out before. That was who he was."

Marcos's phone explodes at 9 AM. Texts, missed calls, news alerts. A neighbor's Ring camera captured Luna and Felix's entire survey of the DA's house — their systematic movement through the rooms, Felix sitting in front of the painting, Luna's paw triggering the frame. Someone posted it.

Marcos
(staring at his phone, horrified)
"Eighty million views. The headline is — 'THESE CATS FOUND THE SAFE.'"
Luna (V.O.)
"We were famous. This was going to complicate things. Or accelerate them. I wasn't sure which."

Marcos scrolls through his DMs — hundreds of strangers, journalists, conspiracy accounts. He almost puts the phone down. Then one message stops him. Username: vee_nowhere. Sent three minutes ago. He reads it. Reads it again.

Marcos
(typing back)
"Who are you?"
⚡ Cliffhanger

The reply comes in under ten seconds: "Someone your DA tried to silence. I recorded everything. I have been waiting two years for someone to find that safe." Marcos sets the phone face-up on the table. Luna stares at the screen. The typing indicator appears again.

Episode 8
The Witness
Runtime: ~85s

INT. QUIET CAFE — CORNER TABLE — DAY

VERA, 40s, small and watchful, sits with her hands wrapped around a coffee cup she hasn't touched. She has the eyes of someone who has been listening for footsteps for a long time. Luna is in a carrier under the table — Marcos brought her everywhere now.

Vera
"I was a paralegal at David's law firm for six years. Three weeks before Elena Harlan died — Elena, victim number two — I was in the wrong conference room. I heard David and DA Harlan talking. I recorded it."
Marcos
"What were they discussing?"
Vera
(flat, practiced)
"How to make it look like an accident. The insurance structure. Who would handle the DA's end. They used names. They were very specific." A breath. "I went into hiding four days later. Someone broke into my apartment. I knew it was a message."
Marcos
"You'll testify?"
Vera
"Only with full immunity. And the DA controls immunity in this jurisdiction. So."

Silence. Marcos processes the wall they've just hit. Then Vera slides her phone across the table. He puts in an earbud and presses play. His face changes — a progression from focused to rigid to something that looks like grief and fury simultaneously.

Marcos
(pulling the earbud, steady)
"When was this recorded?"
Vera
"Three weeks before Elena died."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Luna stretches one paw through the carrier grate and rests it on the table, directly touching Vera's wrist. Vera looks down at the small paw. Then up at Marcos. "Is she always like this?" Marcos: "Only when she trusts someone." Vera stares at the carrier for a long moment — and then, for the first time, she exhales.

Episode 9
The Trade
Runtime: ~80s

INT. DA HARLAN'S OFFICE — DAY

Marcos, in his good jacket, sits across from DA Harlan. Harlan is polished, careful, a man who has never once been in a room where he wasn't in control.

Marcos
"I have the hard drive from your safe. Everything on it. I'll bury it — the recordings, the shell company filings, all of it — in exchange for one thing. Full immunity for a witness named Vera Castillo."
DA Harlan
(a long pause — measuring)
"You don't have the authority to make that offer. You're suspended."
Marcos
"I have five copies of that drive. Immunity. Or I send one to the FBI tonight."

Harlan agrees. Signs the immunity paperwork personally, efficiently — a man relieved to believe he has won. The moment Marcos leaves, Harlan makes a call. His voice changes — lower, older, without the veneer.

DA Harlan
(into phone)
"Marcos has been neutralized. He took the deal. Handle the loose end — the address I'm texting you. Tonight."

INT. MARCOS'S CAR — PARKED — CONTINUOUS

Marcos sits in the driver's seat. Two FBI agents occupy the back seat, headphones on, a recorder between them. Luna is in the passenger seat, watching the DA's building entrance.

Marcos
(touching the wire under his collar)
"Got him."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Luna turns from the window and blinks at Marcos — slow, deliberate, the long blink that means trust. He reaches over and scratches behind her ear. In the back seat, one of the FBI agents says quietly: "Mr. Marcos — we just got the intercept on the address he texted. That's your apartment building."

Episode 10
Double Cross 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT BUILDING — STAIRWELL — NIGHT

Marcos takes the stairs fast, ahead of the agents. The building is quiet. He hits the third-floor landing — and a large man in dark clothes is moving toward apartment 3C with purpose and a key. Luna, in her carrier on Marcos's back, presses her paws against the mesh.

Luna (V.O.)
"I smelled him before Marcos saw him. Wrong smell — not a resident, not a visitor. Intentional. Like David had smelled intentional on that staircase."

The man reaches the top stair. Luna presses through the carrier mesh and swipes — claws connecting with the man's ankle, clean and precise. He pitches forward. The landing catches him hard. He goes down, the carrier skidding away, and his jacket falls open.

Marcos
(crouching, pulling back the jacket — stunned)
"He's wired. He's wearing a recording device."

Not Marcos's wire — a different one. Broadcasting on a frequency Marcos doesn't recognize. The FBI agents arrive on the landing, one already on his radio. His expression is strange — not urgency. Something closer to satisfaction.

FBI Agent
"That frequency — it's ours. He's been broadcasting to a second operation. We didn't put that device on him." A beat. "David did. He called this man from prison to order the hit. And David didn't know we were already listening on this channel."

Silence on the stairwell landing. Luna sits beside the unconscious man, tail wrapped, watching Marcos.

Luna (V.O.)
"The confession was live. On a police frequency. Three murders. The order to kill Marcos. David said all of it. He thought he was being careful. He always thought he was being careful."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos's phone rings. He answers. The voice on the other end is unhurried: "Mr. Marcos. This is Special Agent Reyes, FBI. We have everything — all three murders, the DA, the attempted hit on you. We've been building this case for eighteen months. We needed one more confession. Your cat just got us the last piece." Outside, three blocks away, police lights are already pulling up to the DA's house. Luna sits on the windowsill at the end of the hall, tail swishing, watching the street below.

Episode 11
Justice, Finally
Runtime: ~85s

INT. COURTHOUSE — SENTENCING HEARING — DAY

The courtroom is full. DA Harlan resigned within hours of his arrest — no statement, no press. David's sentencing is today. Three consecutive life terms. The judge reads it without drama, in a flat official voice. David sits very straight in his chair throughout.

Luna (V.O.)
"I wasn't there. I was never allowed in courtrooms. But Marcos told me everything when he came home. I could read it in the way he sat — different. Lighter. Like something he'd been holding had finally been set down."

In the gallery: Marcos, Vera — her immunity secured, her testimony delivered and irrefutable. Serena, months ago in witness protection, has named two co-conspirators in another city. The case has spread like roots. Marcos walks out of the courthouse into afternoon light. Calls no one. Just walks.

Marcos
(to Luna, back in the apartment that evening)
"It's done. All three cases — closed. Reclassified. They're going to add the women's names to the memorial wall downtown."
Luna (V.O.)
"Good. They should have been there from the beginning."

Marcos opens a beer. Drops onto the couch. Luna climbs onto his chest. He reaches under the door for today's mail — and stops. A sealed envelope, no return address, postmarked from a city he's never been to. Handwriting he doesn't recognize. He opens it.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Inside the envelope: a single sheet of white paper. On it, pressed in black ink — a paw print. Nothing else. No note, no name, no explanation. Marcos stares at it. Luna leans over from his chest and sniffs the envelope — carefully, thoroughly. Her ears flatten. She knows something. She looks at Marcos with an expression he has learned to take very seriously.

Episode 12
Luna's Next Case
Runtime: ~90s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — MORNING / INT. AIRPORT — DAY

Marcos packs deliberately — not a vacation bag. A work bag. Case files. Luna watches from the bed, very still.

Luna (V.O.)
"He had traced the postmark to a city four hundred miles away. He'd found a name. He'd booked two tickets — one for him, one for me. I knew, because he got out my travel carrier and cleaned it."

Felix is left with Mrs. Alcott downstairs — the neighbor who has been quietly leaving food out for him for months. When Marcos knocks and explains, she opens the door and looks at Felix. "I've been expecting this," she says simply. She's already bought a cat bed.

Marcos
(softly, to Felix)
"You did good. Both seasons."

The airport. Security. Luna in her soft carrier, pressed to Marcos's chest, watching everything through the mesh — the crowds, the uniforms, the light changing. On the plane, Marcos opens the envelope again and studies the paw print against the window light.

Luna (V.O.)
"The paw print wasn't a greeting. It was a call. Someone out there knew about me — about what I could do, what I could find. Someone needed a witness who couldn't be bought, bribed, or silenced. Someone needed a cat."

The plane climbs. Luna presses her nose to the small oval window. Below, the city diminishes — streets, blocks, rooftops. Then a neighborhood. One house. A lit window on the second floor. A shadow moving behind the glass — fast, purposeful, wrong.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Luna's paw touches the cold glass of the window. Her pupils expand to full black. She sees it — the shadow, the house, something unfolding forty thousand feet below that no one else on the plane is watching. She always sees everything. The plane banks east into the dark. Luna does not look away.
