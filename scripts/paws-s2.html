Paws & Billionaires

Season 2: "One Litter, Two Owners"  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Romantic Comedy / Animal Drama  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: Romantic comedy / Animal drama

Season Arc: The lawsuit is settled. Isabella and Damian are quietly together. Now: Butter is pregnant, the puppies go viral, and a proposal is coming. This season is warmer, funnier, and ultimately the most emotionally satisfying of the series. Biscuit and Butter's V.O. narration becomes wiser — they knew all along.

Animal V.O. Protocol: BISCUIT (Isabella's golden retriever, male, proud dad energy) and BUTTER (Damian's golden retriever, female, dignified and deeply pleased with herself) have full internal V.O. narration — italicized, labeled as BISCUIT (V.O.) and BUTTER (V.O.). They do NOT speak to humans.

Key Characters: BISCUIT (golden retriever, V.O.), BUTTER (golden retriever, V.O.), ISABELLA (30s, sharp, learning to be soft), DAMIAN (30s, controlled, learning to let go), CARMEN (Isabella's mother, delighted by everything), ELENA (Damian's mother, formerly suspicious, now converted), STREAMING PRODUCER (35, enthusiastic, slightly terrified of the dogs)

Tone: Warm, funny, emotionally generous. Episode 10 is the paywall episode — the proposal.

Episode 1
Six Months Later
Runtime: ~75s

EXT. CITY PARK — MORNING

The same bench. The same time. Two coffees. Isabella and Damian walk Biscuit and Butter through the park — unhurried, familiar, nobody broadcasting anything. It's a Tuesday morning in late September and they are simply a fact of each other's life. No announcement has been made. Nobody has said the word "together." They just are.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"Six months. Every morning. Same bench, same coffee order, same slightly-too-long moment when their hands almost touch on the armrest. Humans are so slow."
Butter (V.O.)
"They're not slow. They're careful. It's different."

Butter sits serenely in the grass. She has looked luminously pleased with herself for three weeks. Biscuit has noticed but cannot identify why. Then Damian's phone rings. He answers it — the vet. Isabella watches his face change.

Damian
(hanging up, staring at Butter)
"Butter is pregnant."

Biscuit looks up at Damian with an expression that is, unmistakably, apologetic.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Isabella looks at Biscuit. Then at Butter, who meets her gaze with an expression of absolute serenity. Then at Damian. "How many?" "The vet thinks — probably six." Biscuit lies down on the grass very slowly. BISCUIT (V.O.): "In my defense — I thought she was just being friendly."

Episode 2
Puppies
Runtime: ~80s

INT. DAMIAN'S KITCHEN — 3 AM — NIGHT

Six puppies. Born on a Tuesday, in a nest of towels and blankets Damian assembled with surprising competence. Isabella sits on the kitchen floor beside him, back against the cabinet, knees pulled up. They've been here for four hours. The puppies are impossibly small and gold and everything about them is loud with life.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"I don't know what I expected. But not six. Six seems like a lot of responsibility for one dog. Even a dog of my caliber."
Butter (V.O.)
"I did. I always plan ahead."

Damian, bleary and soft in a way Isabella has never seen him — his tie gone, sleeves rolled up, flour on his knee from a 2 AM attempt at something — looks at the puppies with an expression of pure bewildered love. He's already on his phone.

Damian
"We're basically co-parents now."
Isabella
"Don't make it weird."
Damian
"I'm ordering a second dog bed. And a crib."
Isabella
"A crib. For the dogs."
Damian
"Puppies need structure."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Isabella stares at him. One of the puppies climbs directly onto her foot and falls asleep. She looks down at it. Then up at Damian. "Get the one with the sides that lower." He looks up from his phone. She doesn't take it back. BUTTER (V.O.): "Phase one: complete."

Episode 3
The Families React
Runtime: ~85s

INT. CARMEN'S HOUSE — SUNDAY DINNER — EVENING

Isabella brings Damian to Sunday dinner. CARMEN, 60s, five-foot-two and entirely certain about everything, opens the door, looks at Damian once from shoes to hairline, and says: "You're taller than I thought. Come in." Within ninety seconds he is fixing the kitchen faucet because Carmen mentioned it offhandedly and he just... went and did it. Her abuela approves in the universal language of an extra serving of rice placed in front of him without asking.

Butter (V.O.)
"His heartbeat slowed when they accepted him. He had been scared. He didn't show it. But I always know."

INT. ELENA'S APARTMENT — LATER THAT WEEK

ELENA, 60s, precise and quiet in the way of a woman who has decided everything she needs to decide. She offers Isabella tea. Isabella reaches for it. Elena's eyes move — just once — to Isabella's hands. The hands of someone who works. Something in Elena's expression shifts. Barely. Privately.

Elena
"You were right, you know. About the lawsuit."
Isabella
"Excuse me?"
Elena
"The property boundary. You were right. My son was wrong. I raised him better than that." She pours her own tea. "Sit down. Let's talk."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Isabella sits. Elena opens a folder on the table — original property survey documents, annotated in red pen. "I did some research after the settlement. The parking variance in the original survey — you found it months before you filed, didn't you." It's not a question. Isabella looks at her. "Yes." Elena nods. "Good. I want to know how." She refills Isabella's tea. Biscuit, sitting next to Elena's chair, looks up at her with an expression of profound respect. BISCUIT (V.O.): "I think I have a new favorite person. Don't tell Isabella."

Episode 4
His Mother
Runtime: ~80s

INT. ELENA'S APARTMENT — EVENING, CONTINUING

Four hours. They move from the property survey to the settlement terms to Elena's own career as a property attorney, to a second bottle of wine Carmen has sent over via Damian (who delivered it, knocked, and left immediately because Elena's expression said: not invited). By the end of the evening they are making plans that don't include their children.

Biscuit sits beside Elena the entire time, listening attentively to both of them. At one point Elena leans down and says, not quite to herself:

Elena
(to Biscuit, quietly, while Isabella steps out)
"My son needed someone who would not be managed. I was beginning to think they didn't exist." Biscuit tilts his head. Elena pats him once. "You did well."

At the door, end of the night, Elena walks Damian out while Isabella waits for a car. Elena pats his cheek.

Elena
"I like her."
Damian
"I know."
Elena
"She found the parking variance months before she filed. She had you before you knew she had you." She looks at him levelly. "She's smarter than you."
Damian
"I know that too."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Elena: "Good. Don't forget it." She closes the door. In the hallway Damian stands still for a moment. Then he pulls out his phone. Opens a jeweler's website he has visited eleven times in the past two months. Closes it. Opens it again. Closes it. Puts the phone in his pocket. BUTTER (V.O.): "He's going to take forever. I can already tell."

Episode 5
The Puppies Go Viral
Runtime: ~80s

EXT. CITY PARK — MORNING

The puppies are old enough for the park. Six of them, on a blanket spread across the grass, in various states of adventure and catastrophe. Two have escaped the blanket. One is sitting on Damian's shoe, completely committed. Biscuit is in full tactical mode.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"They need structure. They need guidance. They need — COME BACK HERE. THAT IS NOT FOOD. PUT THAT DOWN. THAT IS A WOMAN'S HAT. PUT. IT. DOWN."
Butter (V.O.)
"He's doing beautifully."

A neighbor on a bench has been filming for 45 seconds. Posts it without thinking. By evening: 12 million views. By morning: 40 million. By the third day: 80 million and a trending hashtag.

Isabella
(scrolling, disbelieving)
"There is a news article about Biscuit. A real news article. With a headline. 'Golden Retriever Dad Goes Viral Trying to Control Six Puppies.'"
Damian
"There are brand partnerships in my inbox."
Isabella
"There's a voicemail from a streaming producer."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Damian holds out Isabella's phone with the voicemail on speaker: "Hi, this is for Ms. Reyes and Mr. Voss — we're developing a new unscripted series and we think your dogs, your story, and honestly just the two of you would be — well. We're prepared to make a very interesting offer." The voicemail ends. Isabella stares at the phone. Biscuit, sitting between them, straightens his spine, lifts his chin, and poses. BISCUIT (V.O.): "Finally. Recognition."

Episode 6
The Show
Runtime: ~80s

INT. CONFERENCE ROOM — STREAMING COMPANY — DAY

The STREAMING PRODUCER, 35, warm and slightly manic with enthusiasm, has brought a deck, mood boards, and three assistants. Biscuit and Butter are in the room. This was not the producer's idea but they arrived and nobody said no.

Streaming Producer
"Fly-on-the-wall. The dogs, the relationship, the puppies finding homes, the whole origin story — the lawsuit, the settlement, the bench in the park. Eight episodes. We're calling it 'Paws and Billionaires: The Reality Series.' The number we're prepared to offer is—"
Isabella
"No."
Damian
"That's—"
Isabella
"No."
Damian
"If we donated the full fee to the—"
Isabella
"Damian."
Damian
"Just think about—"

Biscuit stands up, crosses the room with authority, takes the producer's contract folder in his mouth, carries it across the room, and drops it in the trash can beside the door. Returns to his seat. Sits. Looks at Isabella.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Everyone stares at Biscuit. The producer looks at Isabella. Damian looks at Biscuit. Isabella looks at Biscuit. Biscuit looks back at Isabella with an expression of complete moral clarity. BISCUIT (V.O.): "You're welcome." The producer says, very carefully: "Is there... any version of this you would consider?" Isabella looks at Damian. Damian looks at the trash can. Then back at the producer. "One episode. The puppies. Finding homes. That's it. No questions about us." The producer looks at the trash can. Looks at Biscuit. "Deal."

Episode 7
The Compromise
Runtime: ~85s

INT. FILMING DAY — VARIOUS LOCATIONS — DAY

One episode. One day. Four cameras. The puppies — now eight weeks old, three already spoken for — meet their prospective families in a series of joyful, chaotic encounters. Biscuit supervises each handoff with the solemnity of a man handing off state secrets. Butter watches each puppy go with quiet pride.

Butter (V.O.)
"I raised them well. Each one is going somewhere safe. I checked the humans. I always check."

The filming is going beautifully. The producer has kept his word. Then — late in the day — he asks one question he promised he wouldn't. He aims it at Damian while Isabella is across the room holding puppy number six.

Streaming Producer
(low, casual, pointed)
"Mr. Voss — when did you know you were in love with Ms. Reyes?"

Damian looks at the camera. Then at Isabella across the room. She's holding the puppy against her chest and she can see his face and her expression says: do not answer that. He answers it.

Damian
"The deposition. Fourteen months ago. Biscuit walked across the table toward me, and I looked at Isabella — and she was trying so hard not to laugh that her whole face was shaking." A pause. "And I thought—"
⚡ Cliffhanger

He stops. The producer waits. Damian shakes his head slightly — not finished, just recalibrating. "I thought: I've been wasting a year and a half being angry at the wrong thing." Cut. Across the room, Isabella is very still, the puppy forgotten in her arms, her eyes on Damian's face. The camera swings to her. She notices. Her expression shuts down cleanly. But it was there. It was completely there.

Episode 8
What He Said
Runtime: ~80s

INT. ISABELLA'S BATHROOM — MORNING

The special hasn't aired yet. But the clip — that 30-second answer — has been edited out and posted. Forty million views by 7 AM. "THE LAWSUIT BILLIONAIRE FELL IN LOVE AT A DEPOSITION" is trending in eleven countries. Isabella's phone has not stopped vibrating since she woke up.

She watches the clip alone, phone propped on the bathroom sink. She watches it four times.

Butter (V.O.)
"She cried. Just a little. Standing at the sink with the water running so he wouldn't hear. She would never admit it. But I could smell the salt. I always know."

Isabella comes out of the bathroom. The apartment is quiet. Damian is on the couch — both dogs flanking him, bracketing both sides of the cushion, clearly arranged. He doesn't say anything. Just moves over. She sits. He puts his arm around her — easy, unhurried, like it has been there for years.

She doesn't move away.

⚡ Cliffhanger

The dogs settle across both their laps — constructed immovability. Biscuit and Butter exchange a look that can only be described as satisfied. BISCUIT (V.O.): "Phase two: complete." BUTTER (V.O.): "Phase two was always going to work." Outside on the street, someone is filming the apartment building and posting it live: "THEY'RE IN THERE RIGHT NOW." Isabella, for once, has absolutely nothing to say.

Episode 9
She Cries
Runtime: ~80s

INT. DAMIAN'S APARTMENT — COUCH — AFTERNOON

They sit for a long time. The world outside is loud with notifications and trending topics and neither of them looks at their phones. Biscuit and Butter have arranged themselves so neither person can move without disturbing a dog. This is operational.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"Phase one: occupy the exits. Phase two: wait."
Butter (V.O.)
"Phase three: wait more."
Damian
(quietly)
"You know I meant it."
Isabella
"I know."
Damian
"And you're not going to say anything."
Isabella
"I'm thinking."
Damian
"You think too much."
Isabella
"You don't think enough."
Damian
"And yet here we are."
⚡ Cliffhanger

A long beat. Isabella turns her head and looks at him — really looks at him, the full inventory, like she's making a decision she's already made but needs to make out loud. "I'm terrified." He looks back. "I know. Me too." He doesn't move. She doesn't move. But their hands — both resting on Butter's back — are now touching, fingers barely overlapping, and neither of them moves them away. BUTTER (V.O.): "Phase three: complete."

Episode 10
The Proposal 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

EXT. CITY PARK — THE BENCH — SATURDAY MORNING

The bench. Two coffees. Saturday. Biscuit and Butter are behaving with unusual precision — sitting side by side at perfect attention, not chasing anything, not investigating anything. Isabella is watching a pigeon.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"We practiced this. Butter made me practice for three weeks."
Butter (V.O.)
"He kept sitting on the wrong mark."

Damian sets down his coffee. Reaches into his jacket. Isabella is still watching the pigeon. He turns to her.

Damian
"I've been trying to figure out how to do this for three months."

She turns. He's holding a ring box. She looks at the box. Then at him. Then at the dogs — both sitting with precise theatrical posture, backs straight, chins up. Each wearing a satin ribbon. Attached to Biscuit's ribbon: a small velvet pouch. Attached to Butter's: the ring box's outer case, carried here, the evidence of transport.

Damian
"I'm not going to make a speech because you hate speeches."
Isabella
"I don't hate—"
Damian
"You hate speeches."

He opens the box.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Isabella looks at the ring. Then at Damian. Then at Biscuit, who barks once — clear editorial. Butter barks. A neighboring dog in the park barks. A small child on a bicycle passing the bench shouts "SHE'S GONNA SAY YES" and keeps riding. Isabella puts her hand over her mouth. Her shoulders are shaking. She is laughing so hard she cannot breathe. BISCUIT (V.O.): "This is going exactly according to plan."

Episode 11
Yes
Runtime: ~85s

EXT. CITY PARK — THE BENCH — CONTINUOUS

Isabella
(through laughter, barely able to speak)
"Yes. Obviously yes. You impossible — yes."

He puts the ring on her finger. They are both laughing, and then not laughing, and then very quiet — the park around them going on without them, the pigeons, the coffee carts, the Tuesday-morning joggers. Then there are people. Their regular morning dog walkers have gathered — someone tipped them off. And then: CARMEN, in her good coat, standing on the path. And beside her: ELENA. Holding hands. Apparently they made a plan.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"We planned this for eleven weeks. I want full credit."
Butter (V.O.)
"We planned this for thirteen months. Since the deposition. Biscuit didn't know about months one through ten."

Elena crosses the grass to Isabella. Takes her face in both hands. Says something low, just for her. Isabella's eyes fill. She nods.

Carmen pulls Damian aside. She is five-foot-two looking up at him with the expression of a woman conducting a final structural inspection.

Carmen
"You know she needs the last word."
Damian
"I know."
Carmen
"Always. Even when she's wrong."
Damian
"Especially then."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Carmen studies him. A long beat. Then she pats his cheek, once, firmly. "Okay." She turns and walks back to the gathering like a verdict has been rendered. Damian watches her go. He is smiling with his whole face — the expression Isabella saw at the deposition, the one she has been falling toward ever since. BUTTER (V.O.): "We always knew. From the very first morning."

Episode 12
Forever
Runtime: ~90s

EXT. ELENA'S GARDEN — WEDDING DAY — AFTERNOON

Small. Elena's property, strung with the neighbor's lights, Carmen's cooking on every table. Biscuit walks Isabella down the aisle — ribbon-leashed by Carmen, head high, enormously aware of his own importance.

Biscuit (V.O.)
"I have been preparing for this specific role my entire life."

Butter stands at the altar beside Damian. The four remaining puppies — two found families, two mysteriously still present — are in flower crowns held in place by toddler hands and pure stubbornness. During the vows, Isabella starts to improvise. Damian was not prepared for this.

Isabella
(setting aside her written vows)
"You were my worst adversary and my best surprise. You ordered a crib for puppies at 3 AM and I knew — I knew right then. I said don't make it weird and I already had. I was already gone." A breath. "I love you. You impossible man. You infuriating, thorough, genuine — yes. Always yes."

Damian is doing the thing. The thing he did at the deposition — trying not to smile, jaw tight, eyes bright. He fails completely.

EXT. ELENA'S GARDEN — EDGE — LATER

After the ceremony. Just the two of them and the dogs, for a moment, at the edge of the garden. Their park bench — moved here for the reception — stands beside a flower arch. On its armrest: a small hand-painted sign.

Isabella
"When did you make this?"
Damian
"Carmen made it. Three months ago. She was optimistic."

The sign reads: RESERVED — B & B. ALWAYS.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Isabella leans her head against his shoulder. They look out at the garden — her mother dancing, his mother laughing, four dogs arranged across the bench in the exact formation they've occupied every morning for six months. Biscuit and Butter are shoulder to shoulder, the two remaining puppies pressed against their sides. BUTTER (V.O.): "We always knew. From the very first morning in the park. Before they knew. We always knew." BISCUIT (V.O.): "I knew second. That still counts." BUTTER (V.O.): "Yes. It does."
