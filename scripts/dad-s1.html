Pulse Reels — Original Series Script

My Billionaire Dad Is Also My Boss

Season 1  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Family Drama / Revenge / Identity  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: Live-action family drama / revenge / identity / reconciliation

Target Audience: 18–45 female-skewing viewers; family drama and workplace revenge crossover; Latina-led lead character

Format: Vertical 9:16 short-form; close-up driven; each episode 60–90 seconds. Tone escalates from controlled to raw across the season.

Tone: Restrained, emotionally loaded. Characters carry enormous weight below the surface. Dialogue is quiet and precise — emotion lives in what is NOT said, until it can't be held anymore.

Key Characters: CATALINA (late 20s, Latina, fierce, brilliant, grieving her mother — plays a long, patient game), RODRIGO SALINAS (50s, powerful CEO, composed, carries 25 years of guilt that has curdled into success), JAMES (35, Rodrigo's longtime assistant — loyal, perceptive, quietly notices things before anyone else does)

Season Arc: A daughter uses her brilliance to infiltrate her absent father's company — not to destroy him, but to make him see her, and then decide what justice looks like when it is personal.

Episode 1
The Letter
Runtime: ~70s

INT. HOSPITAL ROOM — NIGHT

A quiet room. Machines. CATALINA sits beside a bed holding a woman's hand. Her mother — early 50s, beautiful despite the illness, the same eyes as Catalina. She is dying and both of them know it.

Mother
(barely above a breath)
"There's an envelope. Inside pocket. The small bag."

Catalina finds it. She doesn't open it — not yet. She just holds it.

Catalina
"Mami. Not now."
Mother
"After. Open it after."

Later. Catalina alone in the corridor. Fluorescent light. She opens the envelope. Inside: one folded note. She reads it. Her face changes — not explosively. Just shifts, slowly, into something harder and colder and permanent.

Catalina
(reading aloud, barely)
"Your father's name is Rodrigo Salinas."

She folds the note. Her eyes go to the window. The city spread below, indifferent. She pulls out her phone and types: SALINAS GROUP — OPEN POSITIONS. A job posting appears. She reads it.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina looks at the posting — "Management Analyst, Immediate Start" — and closes her eyes for three seconds, then taps APPLY.

Episode 2
Day One
Runtime: ~75s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — LOBBY / EXECUTIVE FLOOR — MORNING

Catalina steps off the elevator. Crisp blazer. Perfect posture. She has prepared every detail of this. JAMES greets her at reception — professional, warm, curious without showing it.

James
"Ms. Reyes. Welcome to Salinas Group. Mr. Salinas does a brief welcome with all new analysts. This way."
Catalina
(even)
"Of course."

A large office. RODRIGO SALINAS turns from the window. He is 50s, imposing, silver-templed — and he looks at her. She looks at him. She has spent four months preparing this moment. Her face gives nothing.

Rodrigo
(extending his hand, polite)
"Ms. Reyes. Welcome."

They shake hands. He holds the handshake a beat longer than standard. His eyes study her face.

Rodrigo
(quietly)
"You remind me of someone."
Catalina
(a small, practiced smile)
"I get that sometimes."

James watches from the doorway. His eyes move between the two of them. Something in his expression is very still.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo lets go of her hand and turns back to his window, and Catalina watches his back with an expression she lets herself have for exactly one second before her face resets.

Episode 3
The Office
Runtime: ~75s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — ANALYST FLOOR / RODRIGO'S OFFICE — DAY

One week in. Catalina at her desk — surrounded by spreadsheets, tabs open, notes in three colors. She is going through the company with surgical precision.

Catalina
(on a call, quiet)
"Yes. Twelve million. Procurement. Vendor contracts renewed at above-market rate for seven consecutive years — same vendors, no tender review. It's either incompetence or it's collusion."

She compiles a clean three-page memo. Slides it across a desk at morning briefing. The other analysts exchange glances. The department head goes pale.

Department Head
"You've been here six days."
Catalina
"I started reading on day one."

That afternoon. James appears at her desk.

James
"Mr. Salinas would like to see you."
Catalina
"The memo?"
James
(measuring his words)
"The memo."

She stands. Straightens her jacket. Follows.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo's office door is open — and he is standing at his desk holding her memo with both hands, reading it, his back to her, and he says without turning around: "Close the door."

Episode 4
Getting Close
Runtime: ~80s

INT. RODRIGO'S OFFICE — DAY (VARIOUS DAYS, MONTAGE-ADJACENT)

Three weeks of work. Catalina has been pulled into direct meetings with Rodrigo. She is across from him now — regularly, professionally, daily. She is performing admiration she doesn't feel and competence that is entirely real.

Rodrigo
"The Singapore analysis — you caught the currency hedge our entire team missed."
Catalina
"It was straightforward once you saw it."
Rodrigo
"I've had a team of eight not see it for three months."

He mentors her, which she hates. He is good at it, which she hates more. She is good at receiving it, which she hates most of all.

Catalina
(internal, we hear it as V.O.)
"Don't respect him. Don't."

She opens his desk drawer to get a stapler. Under papers: a photograph. She freezes. It's her mother — young, laughing, mid-20s, standing in front of a building Catalina doesn't recognize. Full of light.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina stares at the photo of her young mother in a man's desk drawer — and her hand, which has been steady for weeks, begins to tremble slightly.

Episode 5
The Photo
Runtime: ~80s

INT. RODRIGO'S OFFICE — MOMENTS LATER

Rodrigo has returned. Catalina has replaced the photo. She is standing at the window when he enters — exactly as he often stands, though neither of them registers this.

Rodrigo
(noticing something in her posture)
"Are you alright?"
Catalina
"The photo in your top drawer. Who is she?"

A pause. He doesn't flinch, exactly. But something changes.

Rodrigo
"An old friend. I found it recently. I should have put it away properly."
Catalina
"What happened to her?"
Rodrigo
(quiet)
"I made a mistake once that cost me someone I cared about. More than one mistake."
Catalina
"What kind of mistake?"

He turns and looks out the window. The city below. The same city he has looked at for thirty years from this position of power he built to prove something to himself.

Rodrigo
(one word)
"Cowardice."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina looks at the back of his head and her expression breaks — just for a single, private, controlled second — into something that might be grief.

Episode 6
Her Plan
Runtime: ~75s

INT. CATALINA'S APARTMENT — NIGHT

Catalina's apartment wall. A private board — behind a closet door she keeps locked. Documents. Dates. Corporate filings from twenty-six years ago. Her mother's original business plan — a copy, carefully preserved. Annotations in Catalina's handwriting beside Salinas Group's founding patents.

Catalina
(on phone, to her lawyer)
"The IP was filed under Salinas Group fourteen months after my mother's original plan was rejected by the same board. Same framework. Same market positioning. Different name on the paper."
Lawyer (V.O.)
"That's enough to file. Catalina, you have enough."
Catalina
"I know."

She looks at the board. Her hand is on the phone. She doesn't dial. She doesn't move.

Lawyer (V.O.)
"What are you waiting for?"
Catalina
(quietly)
"I'm not sure yet."
⚡ Cliffhanger

She closes the closet door on the evidence board, turns out the light, and stands in the dark for a long moment — her hand still on the closet door handle.

Episode 7
Why She Waits
Runtime: ~80s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — DAY

Morning. Everyone is in the weekly brief. Rodrigo is at the head of the table. Catalina is three seats down. James, across from her, watches her the way he has started doing — quietly, with something approaching concern.

Rodrigo
"The Lagos expansion timeline — I want it moved up six weeks. Catalina, you'll lead the analysis."
Catalina
"Understood."

After the meeting. Catalina at her desk. She opens her desk drawer and takes out a small ring — a distinctive piece, her mother's, a turquoise band that belonged to her grandmother before that. She holds it. Then sets it on a notecard and writes four words.

She stands. Walks to Rodrigo's office. Places the ring and the note on his empty desk. Walks out.

The note reads: "Does this look familiar?"

James
(appearing behind her in the corridor, carefully)
"What did you just put on his desk?"
Catalina
(walking, not stopping)
"A question."
⚡ Cliffhanger

James looks through the glass wall of Rodrigo's office at the ring on the desk — and his face goes very still in a way that means he already knows what it means.

Episode 8
He Knows
Runtime: ~85s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — AFTERNOON

Catalina is summoned. When she enters, Rodrigo is seated. The ring is in front of him on the desk. He looks like a different man — not smaller, but older. Every year of the last twenty-five visible.

Rodrigo
(without preamble)
"How long have you known?"
Catalina
"Since day one."

He absorbs this. His jaw tightens once. Then he is still.

Rodrigo
"Why didn't you say anything?"
Catalina
"I wanted you to earn the conversation."

A long silence. He looks at the ring. His hand moves toward it — then stops.

Rodrigo
"Your mother—"
Catalina
(flat, controlled)
"Died. Three months ago."

He closes his eyes. He does not perform grief. He simply receives it — sits very still with it — and that is somehow worse.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo opens his eyes and looks at Catalina — really looks at her, perhaps for the first time — and says: "You look exactly like her."

Episode 9
His Side
Runtime: ~85s

INT. RODRIGO'S OFFICE — CONTINUOUS

Neither of them has moved. The ring is still between them. James quietly closed the door from outside. They are alone.

Catalina
"Tell me."
Rodrigo
(measured, but the truth)
"I was twenty-four. I had nothing. Her family — they were established, connected. They made it clear I wasn't what they wanted for her."
Catalina
"And you believed them."
Rodrigo
"I told myself she'd be better off. I convinced myself of it for long enough that I couldn't undo it."
Catalina
"She wasn't better off."
Rodrigo
(a pause)
"I know that now."
Catalina
"Did you ever look for me?"

The longest pause of the scene. He doesn't look away from her. He doesn't dress it up.

Rodrigo
(quiet)
"I didn't let myself. I was afraid of what I'd find."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina stands and picks up the ring from the desk — her mother's ring, her grandmother's ring — and puts it on her own finger, and looks at him one last time before she walks out.

Episode 10
The Lawsuit 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

INT. LAW OFFICES / INT. SALINAS GROUP — DAY

The filing hits the newswires at 9 AM. James sees it first on his phone. He walks slowly to Rodrigo's office and places the phone face up on the desk without speaking.

James
(only)
"Sir."

Rodrigo reads the headline. Reads the filing. Reads Catalina's name as plaintiff. He nods once, slowly, and sets the phone down.

James
"Legal is asking what response to prepare."
Rodrigo
"Tell them to prepare nothing."
James
"Sir, the exposure on this filing is—"
Rodrigo
"I know what the exposure is. Tell them to prepare nothing."

That afternoon. A press conference, unscheduled, called by Rodrigo himself. Cameras. Journalists. James stands off to the side, watching.

Rodrigo
(at the podium, composed)
"Twenty-six years ago, I built this company. I would like to publicly acknowledge that a significant element of its founding intellectual framework was derived from work that was not credited to its original creator. That creator was a woman named Lucia Reyes. I am acknowledging that fully, and without reservation, today."

A pause. Then:

Rodrigo
"I would also like to acknowledge that Lucia Reyes's daughter is named Catalina. She is my daughter. She starts work here Monday — she's been here for six weeks already. She found twelve million in procurement fraud in her first week. She has her mother's mind."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Catalina, watching the press conference from a screen in a side room — alone — puts her hand over her mouth as the room erupts in sound, and her eyes fill, and she does not let the tears fall.

Episode 11
The Settlement
Runtime: ~80s

INT. LAW OFFICES — DAY

Settlement meeting. No contest from Rodrigo's side — every concession Catalina's lawyer asked for, accepted. Her lawyer keeps looking up to verify.

Catalina's Lawyer
(quietly to Catalina)
"He accepted all of it. The attribution. The back compensation. The scholarship."
Catalina
"I know."

The scholarship: LUCIA REYES INNOVATION FUND. Named. Public. Annual. Catalina signs. Rodrigo is not at the table for this part — he signed yesterday, alone.

Monday morning. Catalina walks into Salinas Group. Same lobby. Different context. James nods at her. She nods back. She goes to the kitchen, opens a cabinet — she has clearly memorized which cabinet — and takes down the coffee she brought herself, from home.

She makes it the way her mother always made it. Specific. Exact. Cardamom and something else. She pours a cup. She picks it up. She walks to Rodrigo's office and sets it on his desk without a word.

Rodrigo
(looking at the cup)
"You didn't have to—"

He picks it up. Takes one sip. His body goes completely still. His eyes close for a moment. When he opens them, he doesn't look powerful. He just looks human.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo holds the cup in both hands and looks at Catalina standing in his office doorway, and he cannot speak — and she watches him not be able to speak — and doesn't rescue him from it.

Episode 12
Monday
Runtime: ~90s

INT. SALINAS GROUP — RODRIGO'S OFFICE — MORNING

A week later. Catalina walks in at 8 AM. She has her own office now — down the hall — but she makes the morning coffee every day. She sets his cup down and starts to leave.

Rodrigo
"Stay."

She sits. He lifts the cup. Takes a slow sip. The same stillness as last week — but this time, he leans forward. He slides a manila folder across the desk.

Rodrigo
"This tastes like—"
Catalina
"I know."

A long silence. She looks at the folder. Then at him. She opens it.

Inside: the original business plan. Her mother's handwriting — youthful, confident, dense with ideas. Pencil marks in the margins. A name on the cover: LUCIA REYES, 1998.

Catalina
(touching the pages very carefully)
"You kept this."
Rodrigo
(quietly)
"I kept it all twenty-five years."
Catalina
"Why?"
Rodrigo
(a long pause — the truth)
"So I wouldn't forget what I owed."

Catalina looks down at the plan. Her mother's handwriting. Her mother's mind, twenty years old, full of the future. She puts one hand flat on the page.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rodrigo reaches across and places his hand beside hers — not on it, just near it — and they both look down at Lucia Reyes's handwriting, and neither of them says anything, and the morning light comes through the window and falls across the page.
