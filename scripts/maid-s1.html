Pulse Reels — Original Series Script

Maid of Fortune

Season 1  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Romance / Rags-to-Riches  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: Live-action romance / rags-to-riches / Latino flavor

Target Audience: 18–40 female-skewing viewers; telenovela crossover fans; romance content consumers

Format: Vertical 9:16 short-form; close-up heavy on facial reactions; each episode 60–90 seconds

Tone: Warm, emotionally direct, tension-first. Characters say exactly what they feel — with restraint giving way to explosion at key moments.

Key Characters: ROSA (late 20s, Latina, hotel cleaner, quick-witted, grounded), ANDRÉS (30s, billionaire, brooding, secretly warm), DANIELA VEGA (30s, socialite, manipulative but strategic), ELENA (Andrés's mother, elegant, perceptive — has cleaned for her without Andrés knowing)

Season Arc: Rosa accidentally attends a gala as the wrong woman, falls into the orbit of the man she was pretending to be, and must decide whether honesty will cost her everything or give her back something real.

Episode 1
Invisible
Runtime: ~70s

INT. LUXURY HOTEL — PENTHOUSE CORRIDOR — MORNING

Marble floors. Silence. ROSA pushes a cleaning cart down the corridor in her hotel uniform — focused, fast, quietly singing under her breath. She is effortlessly beautiful in a way the world has trained her not to notice.

Rosa
(to herself)
"Penthouse, penthouse, executive suite, penthouse — I could do this blind."

She enters a room mid-checkout. On the bed, left behind: a designer purse. Small, cream leather. Rosa picks it up to leave at the desk — and it falls open. She goes still.

Rosa
(staring inside)
"No. No, no, I shouldn't — I'm not—"

She pulls out an embossed card: GALA DU SOIR — GUEST OF HONOUR: DANIELA VEGA. A matte black credit card. And a handwritten note: Don't be late. They'll know.

Rosa
(reading, then looking up)
"They'll know what?"

She looks at the gala invitation. Then at her reflection in the full-length mirror across the room — uniform, cart, cleaning gloves in her hand. She tucks the gloves into her pocket.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa slides the gala invitation slowly back into the purse — and then stops, holding it at the edge of putting it down, her eyes still on the mirror.

Episode 2
What Would She Do
Runtime: ~75s

EXT. CITY STREET / GALA VENUE — EVENING

Rosa is wearing a dress she borrowed from a friend — navy blue, simple, beautiful on her. Her car is on the side of the road, hood up. She stands in heels on asphalt, staring at the engine.

Rosa
(on the phone)
"Mami, yes I'm fine, it's just — no, I am not in a dress, I don't know what you're — forget it. I'll call you later."

A LINE of black cars pulls up to the gala entrance across the street. Rosa looks at her dead car, at the gala, at the purse in her hand. She makes a decision that is clearly terrible and clearly inevitable. She walks across the street.

Rosa
(to herself)
"I'm just going to return the purse. Just return it. And leave."

A VALET steps forward, already moving to her side — smiling, professional, arm extended.

Valet
"Ms. Vega, we've been expecting you. Right this way."

Rosa freezes. The valet has her arm and is already guiding her up the steps. She opens her mouth to correct him — and doesn't.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa crosses the threshold into the blazing light of the gala — a thousand candles, a hundred eyes turning — and the doors close behind her.

Episode 3
The Wrong Name, The Right Room
Runtime: ~80s

INT. GALA BALLROOM — NIGHT

Rosa navigates a room of impossibly expensive people with the survival instincts of someone who has served them all week. She reads the seating card placed at the head table. Her fake name in gold ink.

Rosa
(under her breath)
"Okay. Just sit down. Smile. Don't touch anything that costs more than your rent."

She sits. The man next to her is already there — ANDRÉS, 30s, dark suit, jawline that belongs on a coin. He's looking at her like she's a problem he has to solve.

Andrés
(flat)
"You're Daniela Vega."
Rosa
(meeting his gaze)
"So the card says."
Andrés
"I've met Daniela Vega. Twice. At my expense."
Rosa
"Should I apologize for someone else's behavior?"

A beat. He studies her. Something shifts in his expression — not suspicion, but something sharper than curiosity.

Andrés
"She doesn't usually make eye contact with me."
Rosa
"Maybe that's her problem."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Andrés tilts his head slightly, studying her with the full weight of his attention, and Rosa realizes, too late, that she has made herself interesting to exactly the wrong person.

Episode 4
Andrés
Runtime: ~75s

INT. GALA BALLROOM — CONTINUOUS

Dinner has shifted to cocktails. Rosa is managing — barely. Andrés has been cold, exacting, testing her on business topics she's not supposed to know. She's deflecting with intelligence she doesn't bother to hide.

Andrés
"What's your position on the Santos merger?"
Rosa
"I think anyone who pays four billion for a company worth two-point-eight deserves whatever happens next."
Andrés
(pausing)
"That company was mine."
Rosa
"Oh. Well. Four billion is very — it's very... round."

A beat. And then — Andrés laughs. Brief, surprised, like it came out of him against his will. He looks at her differently now. Like he's been holding something tight and just, briefly, let go.

Andrés
"Nobody has ever called my worst deal 'round.'"
Rosa
"I contain multitudes."

His phone vibrates. He glances at it. His face closes again instantly — the warm thing is gone. But she saw it.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Andrés puts the phone face-down on the table without answering it, and looks at Rosa with something that is not curiosity anymore — and she doesn't look away.

Episode 5
The Real Daniela Calls
Runtime: ~80s

INT. GALA — CORRIDOR / BALLROOM — NIGHT

Rosa slips out to the corridor. Her phone buzzes. Unknown number. She answers.

Daniela (V.O.)
(hushed, urgent)
"Who are you and why is security sending me your photo?"
Rosa
"I found your purse. I was just returning it."
Daniela (V.O.)
"By attending my table at a thirty-thousand-dollar gala?"
Rosa
"In my defense, no one asked."
Daniela (V.O.)
(pause, then calculating)
"How is Andrés Salinas treating you?"
Rosa
"Like I'm a merger he doesn't want to make."
Daniela (V.O.)
"That means he likes you. Stay. Play along. There is fifty thousand dollars in it for you. I need the evening — let's say I had plans elsewhere. I'll explain later."
Rosa
"Fifty thousand — wait, no—"

She turns to go back to the ballroom to leave. And stops. Andrés is standing in the corridor doorway. He heard. How much, impossible to say. His face gives nothing.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa lowers her phone slowly, meeting Andrés's eyes across the corridor — and the pause between them stretches into something that is not just silence.

Episode 6
The Family
Runtime: ~75s

INT. GALA — PRIVATE LOUNGE — NIGHT

Andrés has not mentioned the phone call. He steers Rosa, with a light hand at her back, toward a smaller room. Rosa cannot tell if she is in trouble or deeper in.

Rosa
(quietly)
"Where are we going?"
Andrés
"My mother wants to meet you. She asked specifically."
Rosa
"Your mother doesn't know me."
Andrés
"No. But she's usually right about people."

The lounge. ELENA — Andrés's mother — sits in a wingback chair, white-haired, composed, magnetic. She looks at Rosa and something crosses her face. Recognition. She covers it immediately.

Elena
(gracious, studied)
"Andrés tells me you have opinions about the Santos deal."
Rosa
"I spoke out of turn. I'm sorry."
Elena
"He needed to hear it."

Elena reaches out and takes Rosa's hand in both of hers — a greeting, warm, apparently casual. Her eyes fall to Rosa's hands. The small calluses. The cuticles. The hands of someone who works. She says nothing. Her expression does not change.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Elena releases Rosa's hands and looks up at her with eyes that say I know exactly who you are — and turns to Andrés with a perfectly composed smile.

Episode 7
Tuesday
Runtime: ~80s

INT. HOTEL — PENTHOUSE FLOOR — MORNING

Tuesday. Rosa arrives at Elena's penthouse with her cleaning cart — uniform, hair back, earbuds in. Completely herself. She lets herself in with the master key.

Rosa
(singing quietly to herself, mid-task)
"Just a normal Tuesday. Completely normal. Nothing happened. He is not real—"

She turns around and ANDRÉS is standing in the kitchen doorway in a grey sweater, coffee in hand, staring at her. He wasn't supposed to be here. He is very clearly here.

A long silence. He looks at the uniform. The cart. She doesn't move.

Andrés
(very quietly)
"You clean my mother's apartment."
Rosa
(chin up)
"Every Tuesday. For two years."
Andrés
"And the gala—"
Rosa
"Was a mistake. Which I was in the process of not repeating."

He looks at her for a long time. She holds her ground. He sets down his coffee. He doesn't look angry. He looks like someone whose map just changed.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Andrés steps aside to let her work — and Rosa pushes her cart past him close enough that her arm almost brushes his, and neither of them moves away.

Episode 8
The Real Daniela Arrives
Runtime: ~75s

INT. SALINAS FAMILY HOME — BRUNCH — DAY

Bright Sunday light. Elena's table. Rosa is there as Rosa — Andrés invited her, without explaining why to anyone. Including Rosa.

Rosa
(quietly, to Andrés)
"You didn't tell me this was your family home."
Andrés
"I told you it was brunch."
Rosa
"There is a chandelier."

The door opens. DANIELA VEGA enters — head high, designer-draped, reading the room in one second flat. She sees Rosa. Her jaw tightens. She scans Andrés's face. And then — she reads what she sees in his eyes when he looks at Rosa. She stops walking.

Daniela
(to the room, recovering perfectly)
"I heard there was brunch."

She sits down across from Rosa. She takes a breath. She picks up her napkin. She smiles at Rosa — and it is a real smile, sharp at the edges, but real.

Daniela
(privately, to Rosa)
"I see you've been busy."
Rosa
"I was just returning your purse."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Daniela tilts her head and says, so only Rosa can hear: "He's never looked at me like that. Not once. You should know what you're holding."

Episode 9
Blackmail
Runtime: ~80s

INT. HOTEL LOBBY / ANDRÉS'S OFFICE — DAY

Daniela meets Rosa in the hotel lobby — away from everyone. She is not smiling now.

Daniela
(low, direct)
"There's a merger contract in Andrés's private office. Top drawer, red folder. I need you to bring it to me."
Rosa
"No."
Daniela
"No? I can have you fired for gala fraud by end of day."
Rosa
(a beat)
"Fine."

Rosa gets the contract. She's alone in Andrés's office — access badge Daniela arranged somehow. She opens the red folder. She starts to refold it to take. Then she reads it. The cover page. The acquisition target. The address. She goes very still.

Rosa
(quiet, to herself)
"That's my neighborhood. He's buying my neighborhood."

She reads further. Dozens of apartment units. Families. Eminent domain clause. Her mother's building — she sees the address listed specifically.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa carefully folds the contract, tucks it under her arm, and walks out of the office — but instead of turning toward the lobby and Daniela, she turns in the opposite direction.

Episode 10
The Choice 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

INT. HOTEL — CORRIDOR OUTSIDE ANDRÉS'S OFFICE — DAY

Rosa turns a corner and finds Daniela waiting. She holds out the contract. Daniela reaches for it. Rosa doesn't let go.

Rosa
"What are you going to do with this?"
Daniela
"Stop a deal I don't benefit from. That's all."
Rosa
"And the two hundred families on those blocks? You don't benefit from them either."
Daniela
(edge creeping in)
"Rosa. Give me the contract."
Rosa
(not letting go)
"My mother lives on Calle Nueve. Block C. Line four on page seven."

Daniela's expression flickers. Just once. Then she lets go of the contract. She steps back.

Daniela
(quietly)
"He doesn't know about that building specifically. He's never looked at the addresses. He signs what his team brings him."

Footsteps. Andrés appears at the corridor junction. He takes in Daniela. He takes in Rosa. He sees the red folder. His face is unreadable. Then — very slowly — it shifts into something she cannot name.

Andrés
(looking only at Rosa)
"Rosa. That's your real name, isn't it."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa holds the merger contract against her chest, Daniela stands behind her, and Andrés's eyes move from Rosa's face to the red folder and back — waiting.

Episode 11
The Truth
Runtime: ~85s

INT. ANDRÉS'S OFFICE — DAY

Daniela has gone. Andrés and Rosa, alone. The contract is on the desk between them. Rosa doesn't sit.

Rosa
"My name is Rosa Medina. I'm a hotel housekeeper. I've cleaned your mother's apartment every Tuesday for two years. I found Daniela's purse by accident. I went to the gala to return it and a valet took my arm and I — made a stupid decision."
Andrés
(quietly)
"I know."
Rosa
"I'm sorry I lied."
Andrés
"I've known for three weeks."
Rosa
(genuinely staggered)
"Three weeks?"
Andrés
"My mother recognized you the night of the gala. She told me the next morning."
Rosa
"And you said nothing."
Andrés
(a pause)
"I didn't want you to stop."

The door behind Rosa opens. Elena stands in the doorway, reads both of their faces, and steps back without a word — but doesn't fully leave. Just waits.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa looks at Andrés — at the man who knew, who stayed quiet, who let her be real — and she doesn't know yet if that was the kindest thing or the most dangerous.

Episode 12
What She's Worth
Runtime: ~90s

INT. ANDRÉS'S OFFICE — CONTINUOUS

The merger contract is still on the desk. Elena has come inside. She sits quietly in the corner chair — witness, not participant.

Andrés
"The contract. You took it for Daniela?"
Rosa
"She threatened me. But I read it first."
Andrés
"And?"
Rosa
"Page seven, line four. My mother's building."

He goes very still. He pulls the contract toward him, opens it. His eyes find page seven. He reads. He closes the folder.

Andrés
"I didn't know."
Rosa
"I know."
Andrés
(quietly, direct)
"I'll pull it. The whole block."

Rosa looks at him. A beat. Then:

Andrés
"I want to offer you a position. Development review. Community impact — real authority. Real salary."
Rosa
(a long pause, a small smile building)
"I almost want to say yes."
Andrés
"But?"
Rosa
"What happens when your neighborhood finds out who your company was going to destroy? What does that story say about the man who hired me? Does it look like guilt? Or something real?"

Elena, from the corner, quietly: "It looks like both. Which is usually what real is." Andrés and Rosa look at each other across the desk.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Rosa picks up a pen from his desk — his own pen — and slides the contract back to him unsigned, and Andrés looks down at it and then back up at her with the expression of a man who has just understood what kind of woman he is dealing with.
