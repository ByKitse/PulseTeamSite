Pulse Reels — Original Series Script

The Cat Who Knew Too Much

Season 1  |  12 Episodes  |  Genre: Animal Drama / Mystery-Thriller  |  Vertical 9:16 Format

Production Notes

Genre: AI-generated animal drama / Mystery-Thriller

Target Audience: 18–35 female-skewing viewers; true-crime + cozy mystery crossover fans

Format: Vertical 9:16 short-form; close-up heavy; each episode 60–90 seconds

Animal V.O. Protocol: LUNA (Siamese cat) has full internal V.O. narration — italicized, labeled as LUNA (V.O.). Luna does NOT speak to humans. All Luna communication is through body language (hissing, knocking objects, staring). Human characters speak normally. Dog characters (Biscuit) follow same V.O. rule.

Key Characters: LUNA (Siamese cat, Elena's pet / our hero), ELENA (the victim), DAVID (Elena's husband / prime suspect), MARCOS (detective, Luna's eventual human), SERENA (David's accomplice / the new woman)

Season Arc: Luna witnesses a murder, cannot speak, must guide a detective to the truth before she's silenced.

Episode 1
The Night It Happened
Runtime: ~75s

INT. ELENA'S TOWNHOUSE — STAIRCASE — NIGHT

A luxury townhouse. Marble stairs catch the last of the hall light. LUNA sits at the top landing, tail wrapped tight, watching.

Luna (V.O.)
"Elena always hummed when she came home. Low, off-key, happy. Tonight she was silent."

ELENA, 30s, elegant, hurries up the stairs — phone in hand, face pale with worry. A SHADOW moves behind her.

Luna (V.O.)
"I felt it before I saw it. The air changed. The way it changes before a storm."

Luna springs to her feet. Elena reaches the top step — and the shadow SHOVES her. Elena falls. The phone skitters under the hall rug. Luna FLATTENS against the wall.

Luna (V.O.)
"She didn't scream. There wasn't time."

SILENCE. Then the shadow turns. Steps closer to Luna. A face enters the light — calm. Smiling.

Luna (V.O.)
"He looked right at me. And smiled. Like it was nothing. Like I was nothing."

Luna stares back. Her pupils dilate fully — wide, black, terrified. She doesn't run.

Luna (V.O.)
"I memorized his face. Every line. Every shadow. I am the only witness. And I am a cat."
⚡ Cliffhanger

The shadow crouches to Luna's eye level, reaches out one finger — Luna does not flinch — and then stands, steps over Elena's body, and walks out the front door, pulling it quietly shut behind him.

Episode 2
They Think It Was an Accident
Runtime: ~80s

INT. ELENA'S TOWNHOUSE — DAY

Morning. Police tape. DETECTIVE MARCOS, 30s, tired eyes, sharp instincts, crouches at the base of the stairs. Luna watches him from the landing above.

Luna (V.O.)
"They called it an accident. Missed a step. Elena, who had lived in this house for nine years. Elena, who could navigate these stairs in the dark."
Marcos
(to his partner, quietly)
"No grip marks. No defensive wounds. She just... fell."
Partner
"Husband found her at 6 AM. Story checks out."

Luna walks deliberately down the stairs and stops directly in front of Marcos. She stares at him.

Marcos
(to the cat)
"Hey. You see something, little one?"
Luna (V.O.)
"Yes. I saw everything. LOOK AT THE RUG."

Luna paws at the edge of the hall rug, repeatedly. Marcos watches, then absently lifts the corner — and freezes. Beneath it: a cracked phone. Screen cracked but lit. A draft message visible on the display.

Marcos
(reading, low)
"'If something happens tonight, it wasn't—' ... Message was never sent."
Luna (V.O.)
"He looked at me then. Really looked. Maybe he wasn't useless after all."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos holds the phone up to the light — the unsent text message is visible, cut off mid-sentence — and Luna watches his expression shift from routine to something harder, something that looks like belief.

Episode 3
The Grieving Husband
Runtime: ~75s

INT. ELENA'S TOWNHOUSE — LIVING ROOM — DAY

DAVID, 40s, well-dressed, handsome in a calculated way, sits across from a TV news camera. His grief is immaculate.

David
(to reporter, voice breaking)
"She was everything. I keep expecting to hear her on the stairs. I can't even — I'm sorry. I just can't."

Luna is in the corner of the room. Her tail lashes once, slow. Her ears flatten back.

Luna (V.O.)
"He cried on command. Like a faucet. He'd practiced this."

The cameras leave. David's face resets instantly — blank, efficient. He moves to Elena's laptop and opens it.

Luna (V.O.)
"The moment the door closed, he was someone else entirely."

Luna jumps silently onto the desk behind him. On the screen: Elena's email. David selects a thread — subject line "Re: what I know" — and hits DELETE. Then empties the trash.

David
(to himself)
"Clean."

Luna HISSES — sudden, sharp, full-throated. David wheels around. His eyes lock onto hers.

David
(quietly, cold)
"You need to go to a shelter, sweetheart."
Luna (V.O.)
"It wasn't a threat. It was a promise."
⚡ Cliffhanger

David reaches for a piece of paper and begins writing — slow, deliberate — and turns it so Luna can't see, while his eyes never leave hers.

Episode 4
The New Woman
Runtime: ~80s

INT. ELENA'S TOWNHOUSE — FOYER — DAY

SERENA, 30s, expensive-looking, confident. She enters with luggage. David welcomes her with a kiss — not like a friend. Like something older than that.

Luna (V.O.)
"The perfume hit me first. Sweet and chemical and wrong. I knew that perfume. I had smelled it the night Elena died — on the shadow that passed me in the hall."

Luna goes absolutely rigid on the staircase. Every hair on end.

Serena
(breezy, to David)
"Is the cat still here? I thought you were handling that."
David
"Appointment's Friday."

Serena notices Luna's stare. She walks slowly to the base of the staircase and crouches down to Luna's level — the same way David did. Smiles.

Serena
(whispering, sweet-voiced)
"I know you were watching. But nobody believes cats, sweetheart. You can stare all you want."
Luna (V.O.)
"She was right. No one would believe me. But I didn't need them to believe me. I needed them to see."

Luna holds Serena's gaze without blinking. Serena's smile falters — just slightly.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Serena reaches out to pet Luna and Luna snaps — a fast, silent bite, drawing blood on Serena's finger — and Serena's composure cracks into something feral and brief before she stands, pressing her hand to her chest, staring down.

Episode 5
Homeless
Runtime: ~75s

INT. CITY ANIMAL SHELTER — DAY

A cage. Luna sits at the back of it, tail curled tight, surveying her surroundings with imperial displeasure.

Luna (V.O.)
"He did it. Friday came and I was in a carrier before I could plan my next move. The shelter smelled like fear and bleach and lost things."

In the cage across the narrow aisle: an older BEAGLE, grey around the muzzle, watching Luna with a slow, knowing gaze.

Luna (V.O.)
"The dog looked at me the way old soldiers look at each other."

The beagle's ID tag catches the light. Luna reads the intake card on his cage: "Owner surrender. Previous owner: E. VARGAS." Luna goes very still.

Luna (V.O.)
"Elena's name before she married David. This was her dog. Her first pet. Surrendered years ago. How long had David been planning this?"

Luna presses her nose to the bars. The beagle approaches and they touch noses through the metal.

Luna (V.O.)
"We couldn't talk. But we understood each other completely. We had both been erased by the same man."

A shelter worker passes — doesn't look at them. Luna turns her gaze to the exit door at the end of the hall. It pushes open when the worker enters. Just slightly. Just enough.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Luna's eyes track the door mechanism — the gap, the hinge, the exact angle — and her body slowly lowers into a hunting crouch.

Episode 6
The Detective's Apartment
Runtime: ~80s

EXT. CITY STREET / INT. APARTMENT BUILDING — NIGHT

Luna moves through wet streets. Rain. Close on her paws on pavement, her face angled into the wind. She has a destination.

Luna (V.O.)
"I had one memory to work with. The smell of his jacket. The specific sound of his shoes on the townhouse tile. Cops have a smell — coffee, paper, something metallic. I followed it across four blocks."

MARCOS's building. Luna slips through a propped lobby door and up two flights. She sits outside apartment 2C and yowls — once, loud, insistent.

Marcos
(opening door, stunned)
"...How did you get here?"

Luna walks past him into the apartment like she owns it. She goes directly to the coffee table — case files spread across it, crime scene photos face down.

Marcos
"That's Elena Vargas's cat. I remember you from the scene."

Luna sits on top of the case file and stares at him. Then deliberately, with one paw, she bats a manila envelope off the table. It lands face up — a glossy photo falls out. Luna presses her paw on it.

Marcos
(picking it up, freezing)
"That's... Serena Holt. Why is she in Elena's file?"
Luna (V.O.)
"Finally. Finally, finally, finally."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos looks from the photo to Luna — and Luna holds his gaze with absolute, unblinking certainty.

Episode 7
What the Dog Remembered
Runtime: ~85s

EXT. STORAGE FACILITY — INDUSTRIAL DISTRICT — DAY

Marcos approaches the shelter — official visit, paperwork for the beagle. Luna rides in a carrier strapped to his passenger seat.

Luna (V.O.)
"I don't know how I communicated it to him. I just walked to the carrier every time he passed it, and eventually he opened it. Detectives are trained to follow evidence. I was evidence."

At the shelter, the beagle recognizes Luna. It pulls urgently toward a specific corner of its kennel — won't stop until Marcos crouches and checks behind the water dish. A torn piece of paper. An address scrawled in pen. Elena's handwriting.

Marcos
(to himself)
"She hid this here. She hid it with the dog."

Storage unit 14B. Marcos gets it open. Inside: a shelf, bare except for a sealed bag. Burner phone. A printed document — LIFE INSURANCE POLICY, beneficiary: DAVID VARGAS. Policy value: $8,000,000. A single ink fingerprint pressed to the margin.

Marcos
(voice tight)
"She knew. She knew what he was planning. She was building a case against her own husband."
Luna (V.O.)
"My Elena. She tried to save herself. She just ran out of time."

Marcos photographs everything. Luna sits in the open carrier at his feet, watching the door.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos dials, raises the phone to his ear — "I need a warrant" — and then his face goes rigid as he notices, on the storage unit door, a fresh set of scratches around the lock: someone else has been here recently.

Episode 8
The Trap
Runtime: ~80s

INT. ELENA'S TOWNHOUSE — LIVING ROOM — NIGHT

Surveillance operation. Marcos has placed a small audio device under the living room table. Luna has been smuggled back into the house inside a delivery bag. She crouches behind the sofa.

Luna (V.O.)
"I had been away for three weeks. The house smelled different. Like Serena had replaced every molecule of air."

David and Serena enter, speaking low. Marcos listens from a parked car outside — earpiece in, recorder running.

David
(barely above a whisper)
"The policy pays out in six weeks. As long as they rule it accidental."
Serena
"They will. They have no evidence. You made sure of that."
David
"There's a detective who's still sniffing around."
Serena
"Handle him. Or I will."

Luna doesn't move. She is perfectly still. Recording. In the car, Marcos's face is granite. He has it all. Then — Serena moves to the window. Looks out. Squints at the parked car.

Serena
(cold)
"David. We have company."
⚡ Cliffhanger

Serena locks eyes with the dark shape of Marcos in the parked car — and doesn't reach for a phone; she reaches into her coat pocket.

Episode 9
Gone
Runtime: ~85s

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — MORNING

Marcos enters his apartment. He stops. Luna is gone. Her food bowl untouched. The window he sometimes cracks — open wider than he left it. Or maybe forced.

Marcos
(quietly, to himself)
"No. No, no, no."

On the kitchen counter, weighted by a coffee mug: a folded note. Three words. Hand-printed. Gloves — no prints.

Marcos
(reading aloud)
"'Drop the case.'"

CUT TO: Serena's basement. Stone floor. One small window near the ceiling. Luna sits in the center of the room, composed, scanning the walls.

Luna (V.O.)
"It was a warning. For him. Not punishment for me. They wanted me gone — that was all. They underestimated what gone means to a cat."

Upstairs: muffled voices. David's voice, unmistakable. Luna tracks the sound through the ceiling. She begins methodically testing the basement door with her paw — the gap at the bottom, the hinge, the exact same way she studied the shelter door.

Luna (V.O.)
"He was up there. Confident. They always get confident. That's when they talk."

Back in the apartment: Marcos pulls his case board off the wall. For one beat it looks like he might actually stop. Then he pins it back up.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos picks up his phone and dials — "I need a location on a black cat, Siamese, chipped under Elena Vargas's name — run the GPS" — and the camera holds on his face: not afraid, furious.

Episode 10
The Unsent Text 🔒 Paywall
Runtime: ~90s

INT. MARCOS'S CAR / EXT. ELENA'S OLD HOUSE — NIGHT

Marcos on his laptop. Elena's cracked phone — evidence bag on the passenger seat. He's been through every tech channel. Then: a hit. Cloud backup. Auto-synced at 11:47 PM — after the fall. The phone uploaded before the battery died.

Marcos
(reading from screen, barely breathing)
"'If something happens to me tonight — it wasn't an accident. Check the garden. The garden at the old house on Vela Street. I buried it three weeks ago. He doesn't know I know about the policy.'"

Marcos stares at the screen. Sits absolutely still for three seconds. Then starts the car.

Marcos
(into radio)
"All units, I need backup at 14 Vela Street. Do not approach until I give the word."

EXT. VELA STREET HOUSE — GARDEN — NIGHT

Marcos's headlights sweep across the property — an older house, garden overgrown. He kills the lights. Gets out quietly. Moves to the garden gate. Stops cold.

Marcos
(whispering)
"He's already here."

In the garden: a figure. A shovel. The rhythmic sound of digging. DAVID, sleeves rolled, completely focused, unaware he is being watched. A hole already a foot deep.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos's hand moves to his holster as the shovel hits something solid in the earth — a hollow thud — and David stops digging, looks down into the hole, and says quietly: "There you are."

Episode 11
What's Buried
Runtime: ~85s

EXT. VELA STREET HOUSE — GARDEN / INT. SERENA'S HOUSE — BASEMENT — CONTINUOUS

Marcos steps into the garden light. David spins — shovel raised.

Marcos
(weapon drawn, steady)
"Put it down, David. Slowly."
David
(calculating)
"Detective. This is private property. I'm just doing some garden maintenance."
Marcos
"At midnight. With a shovel. Step away from the hole."

INTERCUT with Serena's basement: Luna has gotten the door open a crack — claws working the old wood frame. She slips through and moves silently up the stairs. Serena is on the phone at the kitchen table, distracted.

Luna (V.O.)
"Her bag was open on the chair. I had maybe thirty seconds."

Luna leaps to the chair. Paws through the bag — lipstick, keys, a folded document. Luna clamps it in her teeth and pulls — the original insurance policy, wet-stamped, original signatures — falls to the floor.

Serena
(turning, seeing Luna, screaming)
"How did you get out?!"

She reaches for the bag — gun inside. Luna bolts.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Serena raises the gun — aimed at Luna's small fleeing shape — and the kitchen walls echo with the metallic click of the safety coming off.

Episode 12
Luna Saves the Day
Runtime: ~90s

INT. SERENA'S HOUSE — KITCHEN — NIGHT

Luna does not run. She turns. Serena aims. And Luna LAUNCHES — straight at Serena's face, a full-body leap, claws first.

Luna (V.O.)
"I am nine pounds of Siamese cat. But I am nine pounds of Siamese cat who watched my person die, spent three weeks in a shelter, escaped a locked basement, and stole a document from a murderer's purse. Do not aim a gun at me."

The gun discharges — ceiling. Serena falls backward. Backup officers, alerted by the shot, breach the front door.

Officer
"Freeze! Hands!"

Serena, sprawled on the kitchen floor, hands raised, catches her breath. Luna sits five feet away, licking her paw. The insurance document is on the floor between them.

Luna (V.O.)
"The document was intact. The evidence was intact. I was intact. Elena would have appreciated the elegance of that."

INT. MARCOS'S APARTMENT — THE NEXT MORNING

Quiet. Sunlight. Luna is curled on Marcos's lap on the couch. He strokes her absently, reading case files. David and Serena are in custody. The garden yielded a second insurance policy — earlier, larger. Airtight.

Marcos
(softly)
"You did good, little one."
Luna (V.O.)
"I know."

His phone buzzes on the armrest. He glances at it. A new case notification — missing person, suspicious circumstances. He tilts the screen. In the attached photo: a grey tabby cat, sitting in the window of the missing person's apartment. Luna lifts her head and looks at the screen. Then at Marcos.

⚡ Cliffhanger

Marcos looks from the cat in the photo back to Luna, and — for the first time — he smiles.
